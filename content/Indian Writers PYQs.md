## Category 3: An Acquaintance with Major Indian Writers in English

#### Q. 1 (TSS-24): The Sahitya Akademi Award winning work ‘The Shadow Lines’ was written by-
> [!tip]- Ruskin Bond
> ❌ Incorrect. Ruskin Bond is known for his children's literature and stories set in the Indian hills.

> [!tip]- Amitav Ghosh
> ✅ Correct! *The Shadow Lines* (1988) is a seminal work by Amitav Ghosh that explores memory, history, and national identity. It won the Sahitya Akademi Award, one of India's most prestigious literary honors.

> [!tip]- Mulk Raj Anand
> ❌ Incorrect. Mulk Raj Anand was a pioneering writer known for his novels about the lives of the poor and oppressed in India, such as *Untouchable* and *Coolie*.

> [!tip]- R.K. Narayan
> ❌ Incorrect. R.K. Narayan is famous for his novels set in the fictional South Indian town of Malgudi, such as *Swami and Friends* and *The Guide*.

#### Q. 29 (TSS-24): The theme of which of the novels of Mulk Raj Anand is the plight of the untouchables in India-
> [!tip]- Coolie
> ❌ Incorrect. *Coolie* depicts the exploitation of a young boy named Munoo as he moves through various labouring jobs, representing the plight of the landless poor, but not specifically untouchability.

> [!tip]- Two leaves and a Bud
> ❌ Incorrect. This novel deals with the exploitation of laborers on a tea plantation in Assam.

> [!tip]- Untouchable
> ✅ Correct! *Untouchable* (1935) is Mulk Raj Anand's most famous novel and directly confronts the theme of untouchability through a single day in the life of its protagonist, Bakha, a toilet-cleaner.

> [!tip]- The Sword and the Sickle
> ❌ Incorrect. This novel is part of a trilogy and deals with a peasant's involvement in a communist-led uprising.

#### Q. 30 (TSS-24): Toru Dutt was a gifted poetess of the -
> [!tip]- 19th Century
> ✅ Correct! Toru Dutt (1856-1877) was a pioneering figure of Indian writing in English. She produced all her remarkable work during her short life in the 19th century and is considered a major figure of the pre-independence era.

> [!tip]- 18th Century
> ❌ Incorrect. The 18th century is too early for major Indian literary figures writing originally in English.

> [!tip]- 17th Century
> ❌ Incorrect. This is far too early.

> [!tip]- 20th Century
> ❌ Incorrect. While her influence was felt in the 20th century, her life and work were entirely within the 19th century.

#### Q. 32 (TSS-24): The fictional town of Malgudi is a creation of -
> [!tip]- Mulk Raj Anand
> ❌ Incorrect. Mulk Raj Anand's novels are set in various locations across North India.

> [!tip]- R. K. Narayan
> ✅ Correct! Malgudi is the famous, quintessentially South Indian fictional town that serves as the setting for most of R.K. Narayan's novels and short stories, including *Swami and Friends*, *The Bachelor of Arts*, and *The English Teacher*.

> [!tip]- Anita Desai
> ❌ Incorrect. Anita Desai is known for her psychological novels exploring urban middle-class life.

> [!tip]- Ruskin Bond
> ❌ Incorrect. Ruskin Bond's stories are typically set in the Himalayan foothills.

#### Q. 35 (TSS-24): Gitanjali, a collection of poems, was written by-
> [!tip]- Sarojini Naidu
> ❌ Incorrect. Sarojini Naidu, the "Nightingale of India," is known for poetry collections like *The Golden Threshold*.

> [!tip]- Rabindranath Tagore
> ✅ Correct! *Gitanjali* (Song Offerings) is the most famous work of Rabindranath Tagore. Its English translation, largely done by Tagore himself, led to him being awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913.

> [!tip]- Toru Dutt
> ❌ Incorrect. Toru Dutt is known for her collection *A Sheaf Gleaned in French Fields* and *Ancient Ballads and Legends of Hindustan*.

> [!tip]- Nissim Ezekiel
> ❌ Incorrect. Nissim Ezekiel was a foundational figure in post-independence modernist Indian poetry.

#### Q. 75 (STS-32, 2011/13): Select the correct option. ‘Swami and Friends’ and ‘The English Teacher’ are works of:
> [!tip]- Mulk Raj Anand
> ❌ Incorrect. His famous works include *Untouchable* and *Coolie*.

> [!tip]- Rabindranath Tagore
> ❌ Incorrect. His famous work is *Gitanjali*.

> [!tip]- R. K. Narayan
> ✅ Correct! Both *Swami and Friends* and *The English Teacher* are iconic novels by R. K. Narayan, set in his fictional town of Malgudi.

> [!tip]- Ruskin Bond
> ❌ Incorrect. He is primarily a writer of short stories and children's literature.

#### Q. 119 (ST-22, 2011/12): ‘The Guide’, ‘The Painter of Signs’, and ‘The English Teacher’ are the works of -
> [!tip]- Anita Desai
> ❌ Incorrect. Her works include *Cry, the Peacock* and *Fire on the Mountain*.

> [!tip]- R. K. Narayan
> ✅ Correct! This is another question confirming the importance of Narayan's major works. *The Guide*, *The Painter of Signs*, and *The English Teacher* are all celebrated novels by R.K. Narayan.

> [!tip]- Kamala Das
> ❌ Incorrect. Kamala Das was a poet and autobiographer, known for works like *Summer in Calcutta* and *My Story*.

> [!tip]- Mulk Raj Anand
> ❌ Incorrect. His works focus on social realism, like *Untouchable*.

#### Q. 122 (ST-22, 2011/12): Which among the following is not a work of Rabindranath Tagore?
> [!tip]- Gitanjali
> ❌ Incorrect. This is Tagore's most famous work.

> [!tip]- The Post Office
> ❌ Incorrect. *The Post Office* (*Dak Ghar*) is a well-known play by Tagore.

> [!tip]- The Home and the World
> ❌ Incorrect. *The Home and the World* (*Ghare Baire*) is a famous novel by Tagore.

> [!tip]- The Serpent and the Rope
> ✅ Correct! *The Serpent and the Rope* is a highly philosophical and influential novel written by **Raja Rao**, not Rabindranath Tagore.
