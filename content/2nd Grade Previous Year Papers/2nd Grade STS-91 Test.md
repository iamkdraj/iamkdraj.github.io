### 111. Choose the correct part of speech to fill in the blanks : (Question No. 111 to 115)
______ and ______ are friends.
(1) You, he, I
(2) I, you, he
(3) I, you, him
(4) You, him, I

> [!tip]- (1) You, he, I
> ✅ Correct. This follows the standard order of pronouns (second person, third person, first person) when listing them in a positive context. All three are subject pronouns, which is correct for the subject position.

> [!tip]- (2) I, you, he
> ❌ Incorrect. The order is considered impolite. The first person "I" should come last.

> [!tip]- (3) I, you, him
> ❌ Incorrect. "Him" is an object pronoun and cannot be used in the subject position.

> [!tip]- (4) You, him, I
> ❌ Incorrect. "Him" is an object pronoun.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> There are two rules at play here:
> 1.  **Pronoun Case:** The pronouns are in the subject position of the sentence (before the verb "are"), so they must be **subject pronouns** (I, you, he, she, it, we, they). This eliminates options (3) and (4) which contain the object pronoun "him".
> 2.  **Pronoun Order:** By convention and politeness, when listing multiple pronouns including the first person ("I"), the order is: second person ("you"), then third person ("he," "she"), and finally first person ("I").
> The correct sequence is therefore: **"You, he, and I"**.
