# Literary Periods & Movements: PYQ Analysis

## 2nd Grade 2011-I

### Q111. 'Child is the Father of Man.' The above sentence contains a/an :
> [!tip]- ✅ Paradox — Correct
> A paradox is a statement that appears self-contradictory but contains a latent truth. This famous line from William Wordsworth's poem "My Heart Leaps Up" is a classic paradox. Literally, a child cannot be the father of a man. However, it expresses the profound truth that an adult's personality, values, and character are formed by their childhood experiences. The child's nature fundamentally "fathers" or creates the adult he will become. This concept is central to **Romanticism**.

> [!tip]- ❌ Irony — Incorrect
> Irony involves a discrepancy between what is said and what is meant, or what is expected and what occurs. This line is not ironic; it's a sincere philosophical statement.

> [!tip]- ❌ Sarcasm — Incorrect
> Sarcasm is a crude and taunting form of verbal irony intended to wound. This line is heartfelt, not sarcastic.

> [!tip]- ❌ Oxymoron — Incorrect
> An oxymoron is a figure of speech that conjoins two contradictory terms (e.g., "living dead," "deafening silence"). While "child father" seems contradictory, it's a full statement expressing a complex idea, not just a two-word phrase.

### Q114. Who are associated with the Romantic Revival ?
> [!tip]- ✅ Wordsworth, Coleridge, Keats and Shelley — Correct
> This option correctly lists the major poets of the Romantic movement. William Wordsworth and Samuel Taylor Coleridge are the key figures of the First Generation of Romantic poets, while John Keats and Percy Bysshe Shelley are the key figures of the Second Generation.

> [!tip]- ❌ Wordsworth, Coleridge, Pope and Dryden — Incorrect
> Alexander Pope and John Dryden were the dominant poets of the **Neo-Classical Period**, which Romanticism was a reaction against.

> [!tip]- ❌ Wordsworth, Southey, Pope and Coleridge — Incorrect
> This option incorrectly includes Alexander Pope, a Neo-Classical poet. Robert Southey was a first-generation Romantic (a "Lake Poet"), but Pope's inclusion makes the group incorrect.

> [!tip]- ❌ Wordsworth, Keats, Byron and Pope — Incorrect
> This option incorrectly includes Alexander Pope, a Neo-Classical poet.

### Q115. Alexander Pope's famous poem is
> [!tip]- ✅ Rape of the Lock — Correct
> *The Rape of the Lock* is Alexander Pope's masterpiece and the most famous **mock-epic** in English literature. It is a quintessential work of the **Neo-Classical Period**, specifically the Augustan Age, satirizing a trivial social squabble in the grand style of a classical epic.

> [!tip]- ❌ Lyrical Ballads — Incorrect
> *Lyrical Ballads* (1798) is the collection by William Wordsworth and S.T. Coleridge that marked the beginning of the **Romantic Period**.

> [!tip]- ❌ Absalom and Achitophel — Incorrect
> This is a famous political satire by **John Dryden**, the leading figure of the Restoration Period (the first phase of Neo-Classicism).

> [!tip]- ❌ Extasie — Incorrect
> "The Ecstasy" is a famous poem by the **Metaphysical** poet **John Donne**.

### Q116. Neo-classical Movement emphasized
> [!tip]- ✅ the observance of literary rules — Correct
> The Neo-Classical movement valued reason, order, clarity, and restraint. Writers sought to emulate and imitate the classical literature of ancient Greece and Rome, adhering to established genres, forms (like the heroic couplet), and principles of decorum.

> [!tip]- ❌ the non-observance of literary rules — Incorrect
> The non-observance of literary rules was a characteristic of the **Romantic Movement**, which was a revolt against the perceived rigidity of Neo-Classicism.

> [!tip]- ❌ the purity of literature — Incorrect
> While they valued polish and correctness, "purity" is a vague term. The central emphasis was on rules, reason, and social order.

> [!tip]- ❌ the function of literature — Incorrect
> While they believed literature should be didactic (instructive), their core principle was achieving this through rational, orderly, and rule-bound forms inspired by the classics.

### Q117. S. T. Coleridge wrote poems dealing with
> [!tip]- ✅ Supernatural elements — Correct
> Samuel Taylor Coleridge was the master poet of the supernatural and the exotic within the **Romantic Movement**. His most famous poems, like *The Rime of the Ancient Mariner* and *Kubla Khan*, are filled with magical, mysterious, and supernatural events and imagery.

> [!tip]- ❌ Nature — Incorrect
> While Coleridge wrote about nature, **William Wordsworth** is the quintessential Romantic poet of Nature. Coleridge's primary domain was the supernatural and the imagination.

> [!tip]- ❌ Urbanization / Industrialization — Incorrect
> These were primarily concerns of **Victorian** and later **Modernist** writers. The Romantics generally reacted *against* urbanization and industrialization by retreating to nature.

### Q118. 'Beauty is truth, truth beauty'. Where does this line occur ?
> [!tip]- ✅ In Keats' 'Ode on a Grecian Urn' — Correct
> This famous line concludes John Keats's "Ode on a Grecian Urn," one of the great odes of the **Romantic Period**. It encapsulates Keats's aesthetic philosophy, equating the perfection and timelessness of art (beauty) with the highest form of truth.

> [!tip]- ❌ In Shelley's 'Ode to the West Wind' — Incorrect
> This poem by Shelley is a powerful hymn to the revolutionary and creative power of nature, but it does not contain this line.

> [!tip]- ❌ In Shelley's 'Ode to a Skylark' — Incorrect
> This poem by Shelley celebrates the pure, unburdened joy of the skylark's song.

> [!tip]- ❌ In Coleridge's 'Ancient Mariner' — Incorrect
> This supernatural ballad by Coleridge tells a story of sin and redemption and has a moral: "He prayeth best, who loveth best / All things both great and small."

### Q123. Which one of the following features is not associated with drama ?
> [!tip]- ✅ Stream of Consciousness — Correct
> Stream of Consciousness is a **narrative technique**, not a dramatic feature. It is most famously associated with the **Modernist novel** (e.g., James Joyce, Virginia Woolf) and aims to represent the continuous flow of a character's thoughts and perceptions. Drama relies on external dialogue and action.

> [!tip]- ❌ Comic Relief — Incorrect
> Comic relief is a standard feature of drama, especially Elizabethan tragedy (e.g., the porter in *Macbeth*), used to alleviate tension.

> [!tip]- ❌ Soliloquy — Incorrect
> A soliloquy is a key dramatic convention where a character speaks their thoughts aloud while alone on stage.

> [!tip]- ❌ Dramatic Irony — Incorrect
> Dramatic irony, where the audience knows something a character does not, is a fundamental device in drama, especially tragedy.

### Q127. Which one of the following literary periods is termed as the Augustan Age in the history of English Literature ?
> [!tip]- ✅ 1700 - 1745 — Correct
> This period is known as the Augustan Age (or the Age of Pope), the high point of **Neo-Classicism**. It is named after the Roman Emperor Augustus, whose reign was a golden age of Latin literature, an achievement the English writers of this time (like Pope and Swift) sought to emulate.

> [!tip]- ❌ 1649 - 1660 — Incorrect
> This is the **Commonwealth Period** or Interregnum, when there was no monarch and Puritan rule dominated.

> [!tip]- ❌ 1660 - 1670 — Incorrect
> This is the early part of the **Restoration Period** (1660-1700), the first phase of Neo-Classicism.

> [!tip]- ❌ 1745 - 1798 — Incorrect
> This is the **Age of Sensibility** or Age of Johnson, the final, transitional phase of Neo-Classicism.

### Q117. Oxford Movement was initiated by
> [!tip]- ✅ John Keble in 1833 — Correct
> The Oxford Movement (or Tractarianism) was a major religious movement within the **Victorian Period**. It is traditionally considered to have begun in 1833 with a sermon on "National Apostasy" delivered by **John Keble**. Other key figures included John Henry Newman and E.B. Pusey.

> [!tip]- ❌ Other options — Incorrect

### Q118. Renaissance means
> [!tip]- ✅ Revival of Classical Literature — Correct
> The term "Renaissance" literally means "rebirth." It signifies the rebirth of interest in the classical art, learning, and literature of ancient Greece and Rome, which had been largely lost or ignored during the Middle Ages.

> [!tip]- ❌ Elizabethan literature — Incorrect
> Elizabethan literature is a *part* of the English Renaissance, but not its definition. The Renaissance is a broader European movement.

> [!tip]- ❌ Puritan Literature — Incorrect
> Puritan literature belongs to the 17th century, specifically the **Commonwealth Period**, and was often opposed to the secular spirit of the Renaissance.

> [!tip]- ❌ Restoration Age — Incorrect
> The Restoration Age (1660-1700) is the first part of the **Neo-Classical Period**, which followed the Renaissance.

### Q120. Which particular literary term is generally not associated with Drama ?
> [!tip]- ✅ Stream of Consciousness — Correct
> Stream of Consciousness is a **narrative technique** primarily used in the **Modernist novel** to depict the inner workings of a character's mind. Drama, which relies on external action and dialogue, does not typically use this technique.

> [!tip]- ❌ Soliloquy — Incorrect
> A soliloquy is a core dramatic convention where a character speaks their thoughts aloud alone on stage.

> [!tip]- ❌ Plot — Incorrect
> Plot is the fundamental structure of events in a drama.

> [!tip]- ❌ Poetic Justice — Incorrect
> Poetic justice, the idea that virtue is ultimately rewarded and vice punished, is a concept frequently discussed in relation to dramatic outcomes.

### Q121. Who among the following is not considered among the 'Moderns' ?
> [!tip]- ✅ Carlyle — Correct
> Thomas Carlyle was a major essayist and historian of the **Victorian Period**. While he was a critic of its problems, his style and concerns predate the radical experimentation of Modernism.

> [!tip]- ❌ T. S. Eliot — Incorrect
> T.S. Eliot is arguably the most central poet of the Modernist movement.

> [!tip]- ❌ Y. B. Yeats — Incorrect
> While his early work was late-Victorian, the later poetry of W.B. Yeats is profoundly Modernist.

> [!tip]- ❌ Virginia Woolf — Incorrect
> Virginia Woolf was a key innovator of the Modernist novel.

### Q125. 'Art for Art's Sake' was one of the features of
> [!tip]- ✅ Pre Rephaelite movement — Correct
> While "Art for Art's Sake" is the defining slogan of the later **Aesthetic Movement**, the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood laid the groundwork for it. Their emphasis on beauty, sensuousness, and artistic detail, often in defiance of Victorian moralism, was a direct precursor to Aestheticism. The two movements are closely linked, and this is the best fit among the given options.

> [!tip]- ❌ Symbolism — Incorrect
> The Symbolist movement's primary goal was to suggest mystical truths through private symbols, not necessarily to champion beauty for its own sake, although the two movements influenced each other.

> [!tip]- ❌ Tractarian Movement — Incorrect
> The Tractarian Movement (or Oxford Movement) was a **religious** movement focused on church doctrine and ritual.

> [!tip]- ❌ Renaissance Movement — Incorrect
> Renaissance art had various purposes, including religious devotion, civic pride, and Humanistic expression, but "Art for Art's Sake" was not its defining feature.

### Q125. identify the following literary movements : walter pater was the initiator of:
> [!tip]- ✅ aestheticism — Correct
> Walter Pater is considered the intellectual father of the English Aesthetic Movement. His book *Studies in the History of the Renaissance*, particularly its "Conclusion," urged the intense experience of beauty and became a key text for aesthetes like Oscar Wilde.

> [!tip]- ❌ classicism — Incorrect
> Classicism refers to the principles of ancient Greek and Roman art, which were the focus of the **Neo-Classical Period**.

> [!tip]- ❌ romanticism — Incorrect
> Romanticism emphasized emotion and nature, a different focus from the "Art for Art's Sake" principle of Aestheticism.

> [!tip]- ❌ absurdism — Incorrect

### Q126. identify the following literary movements : poetry distinguished by its far fetched images and conceit is
> [!tip]- ✅ metaphysical — Correct
> This is a perfect definition of Metaphysical poetry. It is characterized by its intellectual wit and, most famously, its use of **metaphysical conceits**—clever, complex, and "far-fetched" comparisons between two very dissimilar things (e.g., lovers' souls and a drawing compass).

> [!tip]- ❌ symbolic — Incorrect
> Symbolic poetry uses symbols to suggest meaning, but the term "far-fetched conceit" is the specific hallmark of the Metaphysicals.

> [!tip]- ❌ romantic — Incorrect
> Romantic poetry is characterized by emotion, nature, and imagination, not intellectual conceits.

> [!tip]- ❌ modernist — Incorrect
> Modernist poetry is characterized by fragmentation, allusion, and free verse, not conceits in the 17th-century sense.

### Q70. The most important trait of the Renaissance period was -
> [!tip]- ✅ Humanism — Correct
> Humanism was the core intellectual and cultural movement of the Renaissance. It shifted the focus from a purely God-centered medieval worldview to one that celebrated human potential, reason, and achievement, driving the "rebirth" in arts and learning.

> [!tip]- ❌ heroism — Incorrect
> While heroism was a theme, especially in epic poetry and drama, it was a product of the Humanist focus on human potential, not the foundational trait of the entire period.

> [!tip]- ❌ adventure — Incorrect
> The spirit of adventure, fueled by the Age of Discovery, was a characteristic of the period, but Humanism was the underlying philosophical movement.

> [!tip]- ❌ classical antiquity — Incorrect
> Interest in classical antiquity was the *subject* of the Renaissance "rebirth," but **Humanism** was the *intellectual approach* and philosophy that drove this renewed interest.

> [!tip]- ✅ Literature of the period depicted the countryside and the people who lived there — Correct
> Depicting the countryside and common rural people was a hallmark of the **Romantic Movement**. Neo-Classical literature, in contrast, was overwhelmingly urban, focusing on the sophisticated, witty society of the city and court.

> [!tip]- ❌ Literature of the period deals with urban themes and fashionable, aristocratic society — Incorrect
> This is a key characteristic of Neo-Classical literature, especially Restoration Comedy.

> [!tip]- ❌ Respect of classical rules — Incorrect

### Q77. Two of the important characteristics of romantic age were -
> [!tip]- ✅ Celebration of common man and nature — Correct
> The Romantic movement was a direct reaction against Neo-Classicism's focus on urban, aristocratic society. Romantics, especially Wordsworth, championed the "common man" and found spiritual truth and beauty in Nature.

> [!tip]- ❌ Imitating the great writers and their works — Incorrect

### Q78. Pre – Raphaelites popularised -
> [!tip]- ✅ Art for art’s sake — Correct
> The Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, with its focus on intense beauty, sensuous detail, and art divorced from the strict moralism of mainstream Victorianism, was a major precursor to the later Aesthetic Movement, whose slogan was "Art for art's sake." The two are closely linked in their artistic philosophy.

> [!tip]- ❌ Art should be for entertainment — Incorrect

> [!tip]- ❌ Artists should focus on making money — Incorrect

> [!tip]- ❌ None of the above — Incorrect
> The connection to "Art for art's sake" is strong and well-established by literary historians.

### Q49. Which among the following statements related to Modernism is not correct?
> [!tip]- ✅ The working of the conscious-mind became an important subject. — Correct (as in, this statement is "not correct")
> This statement is misleadingly phrased. Modernism was profoundly influenced by Freud and was fascinated by the **UNCONSCIOUS** mind. The focus was on the hidden desires, irrational impulses, and subjective perceptions that lie *beneath* the surface of the conscious mind. Therefore, to say only the "conscious-mind" was important is incorrect.

> [!tip]- ❌ The Golden Bough had profound influence on Modernism. — Incorrect (as in, this statement is true)
> Sir James Frazer's *The Golden Bough* was a key text for Modernists like T.S. Eliot, who used its "mythical method" to structure their works.

> [!tip]- ❌ It is essentially post-Darwinian. — Incorrect (as in, this statement is true)
> Modernism emerged after Darwin's theories had challenged traditional views of humanity's place in the universe, contributing to the era's sense of uncertainty and loss of faith.

> [!tip]- ❌ It was influenced by the psychological works of Sigmund Freud. — Incorrect (as in, this statement is true)
> Freud's work on the unconscious mind was a foundational influence on the Modernist focus on interior psychology.

### Q64. Certain aspects of Romanticism in the 18th century did not include -
> [!tip]- ✅ Tamed and orderly manifestation of scenery — Correct
> This describes the aesthetic of the **Neo-Classical** garden and landscape. The Romantics, in contrast, celebrated nature in its wild, untamed, and sublime state—rugged mountains, powerful storms, and vast forests.

> [!tip]- ❌ The cult of noble savage — Incorrect
> The "noble savage" was a Romantic concept, idealizing "primitive" or "uncivilized" people as being more pure and uncorrupted by society.

> [!tip]- ❌ Increasing interest in nature — Incorrect
> A deep and spiritual interest in nature is a defining characteristic of Romanticism.

> [!tip]- ❌ Need for spontaneity in thought and action — Incorrect
> Spontaneity ("the spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings") was a core Romantic value, a reaction against Neo-Classical restraint.

### Q68. The Postmodern Condition (1986) was written by -
> [!tip]- ✅ Jean-Francois Lyotard — Correct
> *The Postmodern Condition: A Report on Knowledge* is the seminal 1979 work (English translation 1984, not 1986, but he is the correct author) by the French philosopher **Jean-François Lyotard**. In it, he famously defined Post-Modernism as "incredulity toward metanarratives."

> [!tip]- ❌ Michel Foucault — Incorrect
> Foucault was another key Post-Modern theorist, but he did not write this specific book.

> [!tip]- ❌ Jean Baudrillard — Incorrect
> Baudrillard was the theorist of hyperreality and simulacra.

> [!tip]- ❌ Jacques Derrida — Incorrect
> Derrida was the founder of deconstruction.

### Q103. The comment “Most heterogeneous ideas are yoked by violence together” for Metaphysical poets was originally made by -
> [!tip]- ✅ Dr. Samuel Johnson — Correct
> This is the most famous critical statement ever made about the **Metaphysical Poets**. **Dr. Samuel Johnson** wrote it in his *Life of Cowley* to describe their signature use of the metaphysical conceit, which he found to be a strained and unnatural way of combining dissimilar ideas.

> [!tip]- ❌ William Hazlitt — Incorrect
> Hazlitt was a major critic of the **Romantic Period**.

> [!tip]- ❌ John Dryden — Incorrect
> Dryden criticized Donne for "affecting the metaphysics," but this specific, famous quotation belongs to Johnson.

> [!tip]- ❌ T.S. Eliot — Incorrect
> T.S. Eliot was a **Modernist** poet and critic who famously *praised* the Metaphysicals for their "unification of sensibility," helping to revive their reputation.

### Q106. Which among the following statements regarding Victorian poetry is not correct?
> [!tip]- ✅ There was less spontaneous sympathy with emotion. — Correct
> This statement is not correct. While Victorian poetry was more intellectually controlled than Romantic poetry, it was still deeply concerned with emotion. Think of the profound grief in Tennyson's *In Memoriam* or the passionate psychological drama in Browning's monologues. The idea of "less sympathy with emotion" is an oversimplification and thus the incorrect statement.

> [!tip]- ❌ There was a stringent intellectualism colouring the flights of imagination. — Incorrect (This statement is true of Victorian poetry)
> This is true. Unlike the pure imaginative flights of the Romantics, Victorian poets like Tennyson and Browning filtered their imagination through the intellectual and moral concerns of their age (e.g., science vs. faith).

> [!tip]- ❌ There is a more elaborate perfection of form. — Incorrect (This statement is true of Victorian poetry)
> This is true. Poets like Tennyson were masters of metrical form and sound, creating highly polished and technically perfect verse.

> [!tip]- ❌ There is an element of Romanticism in all the Victorian poets. — Incorrect (This statement is true of Victorian poetry)
> This is true. The legacy of Romanticism, with its focus on the individual, emotion, and nature, continued to be a powerful influence on the Victorians, even as they adapted it to their own time.

### Q108. The work of Simone de Beauvoir was heavily influenced by -
> [!tip]- ✅ Existentialism — Correct
> Simone de Beauvoir was a key French intellectual and a central figure in **Existentialism**, alongside her lifelong partner, Jean-Paul Sartre. Her landmark feminist work, *The Second Sex*, applies existentialist principles (like "existence precedes essence") to the condition of women.

> [!tip]- ❌ Modernism — Incorrect
> While contemporary, Existentialism is a distinct philosophical movement.

> [!tip]- ❌ Realism — Incorrect
> Realism was a 19th-century movement.

> [!tip]- ❌ Individualism — Incorrect
> While Existentialism emphasizes the individual, "Individualism" is a general trait, whereas "Existentialism" is the specific philosophical movement that influenced her.
