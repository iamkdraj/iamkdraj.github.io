### 1. It was a civilized and orderly society. The world 'Orderly' used in the above sentence is -
> [!tip]- (1) Adverb
> ❌ Incorrect. An adverb would modify a verb, adjective, or another adverb (e.g., "They proceeded in an orderly manner").

> [!tip]- (2) Adjective
> ✅ Correct. The word 'orderly' modifies the noun 'society'. Words that describe nouns are adjectives.

> [!tip]- (3) Noun
> ❌ Incorrect. A noun would be a person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., "He maintained order").

> [!tip]- (4) Pronoun
> ❌ Incorrect. A pronoun replaces a noun (e.g., it, he, she).

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> In the sentence, both 'civilized' and 'orderly' are describing the noun 'society'. Therefore, 'orderly' is functioning as an adjective. While many words ending in '-ly' are adverbs (like 'quickly' or 'sadly'), some, like 'orderly', 'friendly', and 'lovely', are adjectives.

### 9. When I ..........her house I ..........that she ..........all the five children and ............suicide.
> [!tip]- (1) reached, had found, killed, had committed
> ❌ Incorrect. 'had found' is illogical. The finding happened upon arrival, not before.

> [!tip]- (2) reached, found, killed, had committed
> ❌ Incorrect. 'killed' should be in the past perfect ('had killed') because it happened before the speaker reached the house.

> [!tip]- (3) had reached, found, have killed, committed
> ❌ Incorrect. This mixes multiple tenses incorrectly.

> [!tip]- (4) reached, found, had killed, had committed
> ✅ Correct. The actions of killing and committing suicide happened *before* the speaker reached the house and found out. The Past Perfect ('had killed', 'had committed') is used for the earlier past actions, and the Simple Past ('reached', 'found') is used for the later past actions.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> When describing a sequence of events in the past, the **Simple Past** is used for the main narrative events. The **Past Perfect (had + past participle)** is used to describe actions that were completed *before* the main narrative events took place.
> - **Main Narrative (Simple Past):** When I **reached** her house, I **found**...
> - **Earlier Action (Past Perfect):** ...that she **had killed** the children and **had committed** suicide. (These events were finished before the arrival).

### 14. That the customer gave a false name shows that he was doing something dishonest. Which of the following is not correct –
> [!tip]- (1) "That the customer gave a false name" is a noun clause functioning as subject
> ❌ Incorrect. This statement is correct. The entire clause acts as the subject of the verb 'shows'.

> [!tip]- (2) 'that he was doing something dishonest' noun clause functioning as object
> ❌ Incorrect. This statement is correct. The clause answers the question "shows what?", making it the direct object of the verb 'shows'.

> [!tip]- (3) The sentence follows the pattern SPO
> ❌ Incorrect. This statement is correct. The pattern is [Subject Clause] - [Predicate] - [Object Clause].

> [!tip]- (4) The sentence follows the pattern SPC
> ✅ Correct. This statement is **not correct**. The second clause functions as a direct object (O), not a subject complement (C). Therefore, the pattern is SPO, not SPC.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> A **Subject Complement (C)** follows a linking verb (like 'is', 'seems', 'becomes') and renames or describes the subject. An **Object (O)** follows an action verb and receives the action. Here, 'shows' is an action verb, and the clause "that he was doing something dishonest" is the thing being shown, making it the object.

### 15. Identify the sentence having the pattern - S + V + O + C + A
> (1) She makes the things difficult for me.
> (2) You can teach all students new things easily.
> (3) He sold him the book.
> (4) She put the plate on the table.
> (5) She calls her husband baby all the time.

> [!tip]- (1) 1 and 5
> ✅ Correct.
> - (1) She(S) makes(V) the things(O) difficult(C) for me(A). -> SVOCA.
> - (5) She(S) calls(V) her husband(O) baby(C) all the time(A). -> SVOCA.

> [!tip]- (2) 1, 3 and 5
> ❌ Incorrect. Sentence (3) has the pattern SVOO (Subject-Verb-Indirect Object-Direct Object).

> [!tip]- (3) 2 and 5
> ❌ Incorrect. This was your choice. Sentence (2) has the pattern SVOOA (Subject-Verb-Indirect Object-Direct Object-Adverbial), not SVOCA.

> [!tip]- (4) 2, 4 and 5
> ❌ Incorrect. Sentence (2) is SVOOA and sentence (4) is SVOA.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation

> - In (1), `difficult` describes the object `the things`.
> - In (5), `baby` renames the object `her husband`.

### 16. Rani bought a dress for her husband. Which one of the following sentences given below does not belong to the pattern given above?

> 4. Interpretative and inferential level

> [!tip]- (2) 4, 3, 1, 2
> ❌ Incorrect. This order is also illogical.

> [!tip]- (3) 2, 1, 4, 3
> ✅ Correct. This represents the standard hierarchy of reading comprehension skills, moving from the most basic to the most complex.

> [!tip]- (4) 3, 4, 1, 2
> ❌ Incorrect. This puts the most complex skills first.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> The levels of comprehension follow a logical progression:
> 1.  **Literal (2):** Understanding what the text explicitly says. "Reading the lines."
> 2.  **Reorganisation (1):** Finding and re-ordering information that is explicitly stated in the text.
> 3.  **Interpretative/Inferential (4):** Understanding what the text implies but does not state directly. "Reading between the lines."
