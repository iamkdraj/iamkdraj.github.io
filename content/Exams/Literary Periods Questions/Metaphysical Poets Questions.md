### 1. Who first used the term "Metaphysical" in relation to John Donne's poetry?

> [!tip]- (1) Dr. Samuel Johnson
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Dr. Johnson popularized the term and gave it its most famous definition, but he was not the first to use it.

> [!tip]- (2) John Donne
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Donne was the subject of the criticism, not the critic.

> [!tip]- (3) John Dryden
> ✅ Correct  
> The poet and critic John Dryden was the first to use the term, stating that John Donne "affects the metaphysics."

> [!tip]- (4) T.S. Eliot
> ❌ Incorrect  
> T.S. Eliot was a 20th-century critic who was instrumental in *reviving* the reputation of the Metaphysical poets.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> The label "Metaphysical" was initially used critically to describe a style seen as intellectually showy and unnatural.

### 2. Who popularized the term "Metaphysical Poets" and defined their style as yoking "the most heterogeneous ideas... by violence together"?

> [!tip]- (1) John Dryden
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Dryden first used the term, but this famous definition belongs to a later critic.

> [!tip]- (2) Andrew Marvell
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Marvell was a Metaphysical poet himself.

> [!tip]- (3) Dr. Samuel Johnson
> ✅ Correct  
> In his *Lives of the Most Eminent English Poets*, the influential 18th-century critic Dr. Samuel Johnson popularized the term and gave it this famous, though critical, definition.

> [!tip]- (4) George Herbert
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Herbert was a Metaphysical poet.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> Johnson's critique highlighted the poets' tendency to combine very dissimilar ideas in startling and unexpected ways, a key feature of the metaphysical conceit.

### 3. Which of the following was NOT a context or influence for the rise of Metaphysical poetry?

> [!tip]- (1) A time of intellectual and spiritual doubt
> ❌ Incorrect  
> New scientific discoveries created an atmosphere of uncertainty that fueled the poetry's questioning, argumentative nature.

> [!tip]- (2) A reaction against Elizabethan convention
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The poets consciously broke from the smooth, predictable love poetry of the sonneteers.

> [!tip]- (3) A desire for political stability and national unity
> ✅ Correct  
> The poetry reflects a time of growing instability and doubt, not a desire for unity. This theme is more characteristic of Elizabethan literature.

> [!tip]- (4) The influence of medieval scholasticism
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The poets applied the logical, argumentative methods of scholastic philosophy to dissect emotions in their poetry.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> The Metaphysical style emerged from the complex and uncertain intellectual climate of the Jacobean and Caroline ages.

### 4. What is the most famous and defining characteristic of Metaphysical poetry?

> [!tip]- (1) Smooth, musical language
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is a feature of the Elizabethan poetry they reacted against.

> [!tip]- (2) The Metaphysical Conceit
> ✅ Correct  
> The conceit—a highly clever, complex, and startling extended metaphor between two very dissimilar things—is the hallmark of the Metaphysical style.

> [!tip]- (3) Simple and direct emotional expression
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The poetry is known for its intellectual complexity, not simplicity.

> [!tip]- (4) Idealized praise of a lover's beauty
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is a convention of Petrarchan love poetry, which the Metaphysicals rejected for a more realistic approach.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> The conceit is developed logically throughout a poem, often drawing from non-poetic fields like science, law, or geography.

### 5. In "A Valediction: Forbidding Mourning," what does John Donne compare the souls of two lovers to?

> [!tip]- (1) A pair of stars
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This would be a more conventional poetic image.

> [!tip]- (2) Two banks of a river
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is also a fairly traditional image.

> [!tip]- (3) The two legs of a geometer's compass
> ✅ Correct  
> This strange, un-poetic comparison is the classic example of a metaphysical conceit, which Donne develops through brilliant logic to illustrate the lovers' spiritual connection despite physical distance.

> [!tip]- (4) A flower and a bee
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is a common, natural image, unlike the intellectual nature of a conceit.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> The power of the conceit lies in its ability to make a surprising comparison seem perfectly logical and insightful through witty argumentation.

### 8. What is a notable characteristic of the language used in Metaphysical poetry?

> [!tip]- (1) Elevated, archaic, and "poetic" diction
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This describes the style they were reacting against, like that of Spenser.

> [!tip]- (2) Smooth, musical, and flowing rhythms
> ❌ Incorrect  
> They deliberately broke from the sing-song quality of many Elizabethan lyrics.

> [!tip]- (3) Plain, direct language and the rough rhythms of everyday speech
> ✅ Correct  
> Metaphysical poets often used colloquial language and speech-like rhythms, frequently starting poems with an abrupt, dramatic opening to create a sense of urgency.

> [!tip]- (4) A focus on foreign loan-words and inkhorn terms
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Their language was often direct and even blunt, not overly ornate.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> John Donne's line, "For God's sake hold your tongue, and let me love," is a perfect example of this colloquial, dramatic style.

### 10. Poems like "The Altar" and "Easter Wings," where the shape of the poem on the page reflects its subject, are known as what?

> [!tip]- (1) Sonnets
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Sonnets are defined by their line count and rhyme scheme, not their shape.

> [!tip]- (2) Conceits
> ❌ Incorrect  
> A conceit is a type of metaphor, not a visual poem structure.

> [!tip]- (3) Pattern Poems
> ✅ Correct  
> Written by George Herbert, these are "pattern poems" (or carmen figuratum), where the visual shape of the text imitates the poem's subject. "Easter Wings" is shaped like a pair of wings.

> [!tip]- (4) Allegories
> ❌ Incorrect  
> An allegory is a narrative with a symbolic meaning, not a poem defined by its visual layout.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> This technique is another example of Metaphysical wit, where intellectual ingenuity and form are fused.

### 12. "To His Coy Mistress" is a masterpiece of what common poetic theme?

> [!tip]- (1) *Memento mori* (remember death)
> ❌ Incorrect  
> While the poem is obsessed with the passage of time and death, its purpose is to advocate for seizing life, not just remembering death.

> [!tip]- (2) *Carpe diem* (seize the day)
> ✅ Correct  
> The entire poem is a logical argument for why the speaker and his beloved should make love immediately rather than wait, perfectly embodying the "seize the day" theme.

> [!tip]- (3) Contemptus mundi (contempt for the world)
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This theme involves rejecting the world for a spiritual afterlife; the poem advocates for embracing worldly pleasure.

> [!tip]- (4) Courtly love
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The poem subverts the patient suffering of courtly love with its urgent demand for physical union.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> The poem's famous lines, "But at my back I always hear / Time's winged chariot hurrying near," are a powerful expression of the *carpe diem* idea.

### 15. The use of paradox is a key feature of Metaphysical poetry. Which of these lines by Donne is a famous example?

> [!tip]- (1) "Go and catch a falling star"
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is a command to do an impossible task, an example of hyperbole, but not a paradox.

> [!tip]- (2) "For God's sake hold your tongue, and let me love"
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is a dramatic, colloquial opening, not a paradox.

> [!tip]- (3) "Death, thou shalt die"
> ✅ Correct  
> This line from Holy Sonnet 10 is a perfect paradox. It seems contradictory to say that Death itself will die, but it contains the deeper Christian truth that eternal life will triumph over mortality.

> [!tip]- (4) "A bracelet of bright hair about the bone"
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This is a startling image, but not a logical paradox.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> Paradox allowed the poets to explore complex spiritual and philosophical ideas by forcing the reader to resolve an apparent contradiction.

### 16. In John Donne's "The Flea," what does the flea symbolize?

> [!tip]- (1) The brevity of life
> ❌ Incorrect  
> While related, this is not the flea's primary symbolic function in the poem's argument.

> [!tip]- (2) The physical and spiritual union of the lovers
> ✅ Correct  
> The speaker argues that since the flea has bitten both him and his beloved, their blood is already mingled inside it. He uses this as a witty, outrageous argument that they are, in a sense, already married and should proceed to physical union.

> [!tip]- (3) The sinfulness of physical desire
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The poem is an argument *for* physical desire, not against it.

> [!tip]- (4) A minor annoyance
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The poem elevates the flea from a minor annoyance to the central pillar of a complex philosophical and seductive argument.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> "The Flea" is a prime example of a Metaphysical poem structured as a clever, logical argument, using a seemingly trivial object to explore a serious theme.

### 18. Why did the Metaphysical poets react against the Elizabethan sonneteers?

> [!tip]- (1) They found the sonnet form too difficult.
> ❌ Incorrect  
> They were highly skilled poets; Donne himself wrote many brilliant sonnets. They objected to the style, not the form.

> [!tip]- (2) They found the conventions of love poetry predictable and unrealistic.
> ✅ Correct  
> They grew tired of the sugar-coated, clichéd praise of an idealized beloved found in much Elizabethan poetry and aimed for a more psychologically complex, realistic, and intellectually honest approach.

> [!tip]- (3) They disagreed with the patriotic themes of Elizabethan poetry.
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Their focus was primarily on personal themes of love, death, and religion, not politics in this way.

> [!tip]- (4) They preferred to write in Latin rather than English.
> ❌ Incorrect  
> They were masters of the English language.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> Instead of simply praising a lover, a Metaphysical poet might analyze, argue with, or even berate them, treating love as a complex experience to be dissected by reason.

### 19. The Metaphysical poets' argumentative style was influenced by which field of medieval study?

> [!tip]- (1) Alchemy
> ❌ Incorrect  
> While alchemy appears in their imagery, it was not the source of their argumentative method.

> [!tip]- (2) Scholasticism
> ✅ Correct  
> Many of the poets were highly educated and familiar with medieval scholastic philosophy, which used logic and rigorous debate to explore theological questions. They applied this method to their poetry.

> [!tip]- (3) Cartography
> ❌ Incorrect  
> Maps and geography provided imagery for conceits but did not influence the overall style.

> [!tip]- (4) Courtly Romance
> ❌ Incorrect  
> They were reacting against the conventions of courtly romance, not being influenced by its methods.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> This is why Metaphysical poems often read like a logical proof or a legal argument, using reason to explore deeply emotional subjects.

> Yeats was a major modernist poet, but not the key figure in this specific critical revival.
