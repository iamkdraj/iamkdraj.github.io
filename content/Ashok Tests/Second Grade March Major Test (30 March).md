### 116. My friend has sent me Diwali Greetings.
*(This question asks to identify the sentence pattern.)*

> [!tip]- (1) S V Oi (indirect object) Od (direct object)
> ✅ Correct. The sentence pattern is Subject-Verb-Indirect Object-Direct Object.
> - **S (Subject):** My friend
> - **V (Verb):** has sent
> - **Oi (Indirect Object):** me (the recipient)
> - **Od (Direct Object):** Diwali Greetings (the thing that was sent)

> [!tip]- (2) SVCA
> ❌ Incorrect. There is no complement or adverbial in this core pattern.

> [!tip]- (3) S V Oi (indirect object) C
> ❌ Incorrect. "Diwali Greetings" is a direct object, not a complement.

> [!tip]- (4) S V Od (direct object) C
> ❌ Incorrect. "me" is an indirect object.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> This sentence uses a ditransitive verb ('send') which can take two objects: a direct object and an indirect object.
> - To find the **Direct Object (Od)**, ask "What was sent?". The answer is "Diwali Greetings."
> - To find the **Indirect Object (Oi)**, ask "To whom or for whom was it sent?". The answer is "me."
> The structure is therefore Subject + Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object, or **SVOiOd**.

### 146. Which of the following statement/s is/are true?

> ❌ Incorrect. (A) is also a valid principle.

### 147. Which characteristic of language tests is correctly matched with its description?

> [!tip]- (1) Objectivity - Test consists of objective type test items
> ❌ Incorrect. While objective items (like multiple-choice) contribute to objectivity, objectivity itself means that the scoring is not influenced by the scorer's judgment. A subjective test (like an essay) can be scored objectively if there is a very detailed rubric.

> [!tip]- (2) Validity - Test yields consistent results
> ❌ Incorrect. This is the definition of **Reliability**.

> [!tip]- (3) Comprehensiveness - Test covers a wide range of content as well as skill
> ✅ Correct. Comprehensiveness (or content validity) refers to the extent to which a test samples the entire domain it is supposed to cover.

> [!tip]- (4) Reliability - Test measures what it is supposed to measure
> ❌ Incorrect. This is the definition of **Validity**.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation

> - **Validity:** Does the test measure what it claims to measure?
> - **Reliability:** Does the test produce consistent and stable scores over time?
> - **Objectivity:** Is the scoring free from the subjective judgment of the scorer?
> - **Comprehensiveness:** Does the test adequately cover the content and skills it is designed to assess?
> Option (3) provides the only correct match between the term and its definition. Options (2) and (4) have swapped the definitions of validity and reliability.
