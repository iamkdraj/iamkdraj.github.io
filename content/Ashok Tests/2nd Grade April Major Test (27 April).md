### 11. Change the following affirmative sentence into negative. As soon as the thief saw the policeman he ran away.
> [!tip]- (1) No sooner did the thief see the policeman than he ran away.
> ✅ Correct. This is the standard and grammatically perfect transformation. It uses `No sooner`, inversion (`did...see`), and the conjunction `than`.

> [!tip]- (2) No sooner did the thief saw the policeman then he ran away.
> ❌ Incorrect. It incorrectly uses `saw` instead of the base form `see` after `did`, and `then` instead of `than`.

> [!tip]- (3) No sooner did the thief see the policeman he ran away.
> ❌ Incorrect. It is missing the required conjunction `than`.

> [!tip]- (4) No sooner did the policeman see the thief then he ran away.
> ❌ Incorrect. This reverses the subject and uses 'then' instead of 'than'.

> [!info]- 📘 In-depth Explanation
> To transform a sentence from `As soon as...` to a negative form, the `No sooner...than...` structure is used. The key rules are:
> 1.  Begin with `No sooner`.
> 2.  Invert the subject and auxiliary verb in the first clause (e.g., `did the thief see...`).
> 3.  Use the base form of the verb after `did`.
> 4.  Connect the clauses with `than`.
