Post-Modernism is a broad and complex literary and cultural movement that emerged after **World War II (c. 1945)**. It is not just a continuation of Modernism but a critical, skeptical, and often playful reaction *against* it. While Modernists mourned the loss of meaning in a fragmented world, Post-Modernists tend to **accept that chaos and fragmentation are the new reality** and often celebrate or play with it. The term 'postmodern' was first coined by historian **Arnold Toynbee**.

---

### Core Principles and Philosophy

*   **Rejection of Grand Narratives (Metanarratives)**: This is the core idea, famously articulated by **Jean-François Lyotard**. Post-Modernism is defined by **"incredulity toward metanarratives."** It is a deep skepticism of any single theory (religious, political, or scientific) that claims to offer a universal, totalizing explanation for the world.
*   **Blurring of Boundaries**: Post-Modernism delights in breaking down distinctions between high and low culture (using elements from pop culture, cartoons, and advertising), fact and fiction, and reality and simulation.
*   **Pastiche**: The playful imitation or "pasting together" of multiple, often unrelated, styles and genres. Unlike parody, it does not mock the original style but simply adopts it, often described as a "blank parody."
*   **Irony, Playfulness, and Black Humor**: Faced with a world that seems absurd, the Post-Modern response is often not despair, but irony and play.
*   **Metafiction**: This is **fiction that is self-consciously about fiction**. The work deliberately reminds the reader that they are reading a work of art, exposing the artificiality of storytelling.
    > [!note]- Historiographic Metafiction
    > A term coined by **Linda Hutcheon**. This refers to novels that use metafictional techniques to challenge the idea of historical truth, suggesting that history itself is just another form of storytelling. John Fowles' *The French Lieutenant's Woman* is a key example.

*   **Hyperreality and Simulacra**: Concepts from theorist **Jean Baudrillard**. He argued that in our media-saturated world, the difference between the "real" and the "simulation" has collapsed. We live in a world of *simulacra*—copies without an original.
*   **Unreliable Narration and Temporal Distortion**: Narratives are often unstable, with narrators whose credibility is questionable and chronologies that are non-linear and fragmented.

### Key Figures and Thinkers

The line between "author" and "theorist" often blurs in this period.

*   **Key Theorists**:
    *   **Jean-François Lyotard**: Defined Post-Modernism as the rejection of "metanarratives."
    *   **Jacques Derrida**: The father of **Deconstruction**.
        > [!note]- What is Deconstruction?
        > Deconstruction is a method of critical analysis developed by Jacques Derrida. It aims to show that language is inherently unstable and that texts are full of internal contradictions and hidden assumptions, thus "dismantling" any claim to a single, stable meaning.
    *   **Michel Foucault**: A philosopher who studied the relationship between power, knowledge, and social institutions.
    *   **Jean Baudrillard**: Theorist of hyperreality and the simulacrum.
    *   **Roland Barthes**, **Ihab Hassan**, **Fredric Jameson**, **Linda Hutcheon**.

*   **Key Playwrights: The Theatre of the Absurd**:
    This dramatic movement is a perfect artistic expression of Post-Modern and Existentialist ideas.
    *   **Samuel Beckett**: *Key Work:* ***Waiting for Godot***.
    *   **Harold Pinter**: Known for his "comedies of menace."

*   **Key Novelists**:
    *   **Kurt Vonnegut**: Used black humor and science fiction in ***Slaughterhouse-Five***.
    *   **Joseph Heller**: Famous for the satirical anti-war novel ***Catch-22***.
    *   **John Fowles**: Known for metafictional novels like ***The French Lieutenant's Woman***.
    *   **John Barth**: An American writer known for his highly metafictional works like *The Sot-Weed Factor*.
    *   **Thomas Pynchon**: Wrote dense, complex novels like *Gravity's Rainbow*.
    *   **Italo Calvino**: An Italian writer famous for experimental works like *If on a winter's night a traveler*.
    *   **Salman Rushdie**: A major practitioner of magic realism (*Midnight's Children*).
    *   **Angela Carter**: A British writer whose work uses pastiche and magic realism to deconstruct fairy tales (*The Bloody Chamber*).
