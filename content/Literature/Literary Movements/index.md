# 🎭 Literary Movements

Explore the major artistic and intellectual movements that have influenced literary expression throughout history, understanding their philosophies, techniques, and cultural impact.

## 🌟 Major Literary Movements

### 📚 Movement Overview

| Movement | Period | Core Philosophy | Key Characteristics |
|----------|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **[[The Romanticism Movement (c. 1798 – 1837)]]** | 1798-1837 | Emotion & Nature | Individual expression, sublime experience |
| **[[Aestheticism]]** | 1880s-1890s | Art for Art's Sake | Pure beauty, artistic autonomy |
| **[[The Realism Movement (c. 1850s – 1900)]]** | 1850s-1900 | Truth & Objectivity | Social documentation, ordinary life |
| **[[The Symbolist Movement (c. 1850s – 1900)]]** | 1850s-1900 | Symbolic Expression | Indirect meaning, mystical themes |
| **[[The Modernist Movement (c. 1901 – 1945)]]** | 1901-1945 | Formal Innovation | Experimental techniques, fragmentation |
| **[[The Post-Modernism Movement (c. 1945 – Present)]]** | 1945-Present | Questioning Truth | Metafiction, cultural critique |

## 🎨 Specialized Movements

### 🔍 Distinctive Artistic Schools

#### **[[The Pre-Raphaelite Movement (founded 1848)]]**
- **Foundation**: 1848 Brotherhood
- **Philosophy**: Return to medieval artistic values
- **Literature**: Symbolic poetry, medieval themes
- **Key Figures**: Dante Gabriel Rossetti, Christina Rossetti

#### **[[The Gothic Movement (c. 1764 – 1820s)]]**
- **Origins**: Horace Walpole's "Castle of Otranto" (1764)
- **Themes**: Terror, supernatural, psychological horror
- **Evolution**: From Gothic novels to modern horror
- **Legacy**: Continued influence on contemporary literature

---

## 🧠 Philosophical Movements

### 💭 Intellectual & Existential Schools

#### **[[Existentialism (c. 19th-20th Century)]]**
- **Core Concept**: Individual existence and choice
- **Literary Expression**: Absurdist themes, alienation
- **Key Themes**: Freedom, responsibility, authenticity
- **Influence**: Modern drama and fiction

#### **[[Stream of Consciousness]]**
- **Technique**: Interior psychological reality
- **Pioneers**: James Joyce, Virginia Woolf
- **Method**: Unstructured thought representation
- **Impact**: Modernist narrative revolution

## 🎯 Movement Characteristics

### 🌟 Artistic Evolution

#### **19th Century Movements**

**Romanticism (1798-1837)**
- Rejection of neoclassical rules
- Emphasis on emotion and imagination
- Nature as spiritual force
- Individual creative expression

**Realism (1850s-1900)**
- Objective representation of life
- Social and political awareness
- Working-class subjects
- Scientific approach to literature

**Symbolism (1850s-1900)**
- Indirect expression of ideas
- Musical language patterns
- Mystical and spiritual themes
- Influence on modernist poetry

#### **20th Century Movements**

**Modernism (1901-1945)**
- Formal experimentation
- Fragmented narrative structures
- Psychological complexity
- Cultural pessimism

**Postmodernism (1945-Present)**
- Questioning of grand narratives
- Self-referential techniques
- Cultural criticism
- Blending of high and popular culture
