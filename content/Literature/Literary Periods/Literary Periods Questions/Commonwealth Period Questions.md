### 1. What is the alternative name for the Commonwealth Period?

> [!tip]- (1) The Puritan Age
> ❌ Incorrect  
> While accurate in description, the formal alternative name is The Interregnum.

> [!tip]- (2) The Caroline Age
> ❌ Incorrect  
> This was the period *before* the Commonwealth, under King Charles I.

> [!tip]- (3) The Interregnum
> ✅ Correct  
> The period is also known as the Interregnum, which is Latin for "between reigns," marking the time between King Charles I and King Charles II.

> [!tip]- (4) The Restoration
> ❌ Incorrect  
> The Restoration is the period that *followed* the Commonwealth, beginning in 1660.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
> This 11-year period (1649–1660) began with the execution of Charles I and ended with the restoration of his son, Charles II.

> [!tip]- (2) The imposition of censorship

> [!tip]- (3) The closing of the theatres in 1642
> ✅ Correct

> ❌ Incorrect

> [!tip]- (3) *Samson Agonistes*

> ❌ Incorrect  
> *Lycidas* is a pastoral elegy, not a drama subject to the unities.

> [!info]- 📘 Related Concepts (click to expand)
