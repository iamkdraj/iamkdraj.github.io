# Metafiction

**Metafiction** is a literary device used to self-consciously and systematically draw attention to a work's status as an artifact, to foreground the process of its own construction, and to explore the relationship between fiction and reality. It is, in essence, **fiction about fiction**.

## Characteristics

*   **Self-Referentiality**: The work refers to itself as a fictional construct, often breaking the fourth wall.
*   **Exposure of Artifice**: It reveals the conventions and techniques of storytelling, reminding the reader that they are reading a fabricated narrative.
*   **Questioning Reality**: Metafiction often blurs the lines between the fictional world and the real world, prompting readers to consider the nature of truth and reality.
*   **Authorial Intrusion**: The author might directly address the reader, comment on the writing process, or even appear as a character within the story.
*   **Intertextuality**: Often employs [[Intertextuality]] by referencing other literary works, genres, or authors, further highlighting its constructed nature.

## Significance in Literature

Metafiction became particularly prominent in [[The Post-Modernism Movement (c. 1945 – Present)]], where it served to challenge traditional notions of authorship, originality, and objective truth. It reflects a postmodern skepticism towards grand narratives and a playful engagement with the act of storytelling itself.

## Examples

*   **John Fowles's *The French Lieutenant's Woman***: The narrator frequently interrupts the story to discuss the process of writing the novel, the choices he makes, and the nature of historical fiction.
*   **Italo Calvino's *If on a winter's night a traveler***: The novel is about a reader attempting to read a novel titled *If on a winter's night a traveler*, and each chapter begins with a new first chapter of a different fictional book.
*   **Kurt Vonnegut's *Slaughterhouse-Five***: The author inserts himself into the narrative, commenting on the events and his struggle to write the book.
