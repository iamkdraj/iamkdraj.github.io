## Definition
The **University Wits** were a group of late 16th-century English playwrights and pamphleteers who were educated at the universities of Oxford or Cambridge. They transformed English drama with their sophisticated plots, powerful language, and innovative theatrical techniques.

## Definition
The **University Wits** were a group of late 16th-century English playwrights and pamphleteers who were educated at the universities of Oxford or Cambridge. They transformed English drama with their sophisticated plots, powerful language, and innovative theatrical techniques.

## Historical Context
- **Period**: Late 16th century (primarily 1580s-1590s)
- **Education**: All were university graduates, unusual for playwrights of the time
- **Role**: Paved the way for Shakespeare and the golden age of English drama
- **Innovation**: Brought scholarly sophistication to popular theater

## Key Characteristics
- **Educational Background**: University training in classics, rhetoric, and literature
- **Literary Sophistication**: Elevated the artistic level of English drama
- **Classical Influence**: Drew heavily on Greek and Roman models
- **Blank Verse**: Pioneered and perfected the use of blank verse in drama
- **Complex Plots**: Created more intricate and psychologically sophisticated stories

## Major Members
1. **Christopher Marlowe** (1564-1593)
   - Greatest of the University Wits
   - Perfected blank verse into the "mighty line"
   - Works: *Dr. Faustus*, *Tamburlaine the Great*

2. **Thomas Kyd** (1558-1594)
   - Famous for *The Spanish Tragedy*
   - Established the revenge tragedy genre

3. **Robert Greene** (1558-1592)
   - Wrote both plays and prose romances
   - Famous for attacking Shakespeare as an "upstart crow"

4. **John Lyly** (1553-1606)
   - Court dramatist and prose writer
   - Created the euphuistic style

5. **George Peele** (1556-1596)
   - Poet and dramatist
   - Known for lyrical and romantic plays

6. **Thomas Nashe** (1567-1601)
   - Satirist and pamphleteer
   - Known for *The Unfortunate Traveller*

## Literary Contributions
- **Blank Verse**: Established as the standard meter for English drama
- **Tragic Heroes**: Created complex protagonists with fatal flaws
- **Revenge Tragedy**: Developed this popular dramatic genre
- **Secular Drama**: Moved away from religious morality plays
- **Psychological Realism**: Explored human nature and motivation

## Influence on Shakespeare
- **Dramatic Techniques**: Shakespeare built upon their innovations
- **Blank Verse**: Perfected their use of unrhymed iambic pentameter
- **Character Development**: Developed their psychological insights further
- **Genre Establishment**: Used their genres (tragedy, comedy, history) as foundations
