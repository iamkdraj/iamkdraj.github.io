# Industrial Revolution

The **Industrial Revolution** was a period of major technological, economic, and social change that began in Great Britain in the mid-18th century and spread to other parts of the world. It was characterized by the development of new machines and manufacturing processes, the growth of factories, and the rise of a new working class.

## Key Developments

*   **Textile Industry**: The invention of the spinning jenny, the water frame, and the power loom revolutionized the production of textiles.
*   **Steam Power**: The development of the steam engine provided a new source of power for factories and transportation.
*   **Iron and Coal**: The increased use of iron and coal was essential for the growth of industry.

## Social and Cultural Impact

The Industrial Revolution had a profound impact on society and culture. It led to:

*   **Urbanization**: The growth of factories led to a massive migration of people from rural areas to cities.
*   **New Social Classes**: The Industrial Revolution created a new industrial working class and a new industrial middle class.
*   **Social Problems**: The rapid growth of cities led to overcrowding, poverty, and disease.
*   **New Ideas**: The Industrial Revolution gave rise to new ideas about economics, politics, and society, such as socialism and communism.

## The Industrial Revolution in Literature

The Industrial Revolution was a major theme in the literature of the [[The Romantic Period (c. 1798 – 1837)]] and the [[The Victorian Period (c. 1837 – 1901)]]. Many writers were critical of the social and environmental consequences of industrialization, while others celebrated the new technologies and the progress they brought.
