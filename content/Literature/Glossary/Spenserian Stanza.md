## Definition
The **Spenserian Stanza** is a nine-line verse form created by Edmund Spenser specifically for his epic poem *The Faerie Queene*. It consists of eight lines of iambic pentameter followed by a final line of iambic hexameter (called an alexandrine).

## Definition
The **Spenserian Stanza** is a nine-line verse form created by Edmund Spenser specifically for his epic poem *The Faerie Queene*. It consists of eight lines of iambic pentameter followed by a final line of iambic hexameter (called an alexandrine).

## Structure
- **Lines 1-8**: Iambic pentameter (10 syllables each)
- **Line 9**: Iambic hexameter/alexandrine (12 syllables)
- **Rhyme Scheme**: ababbcbcc

## Example Pattern
```
Line 1: a (10 syllables)
Line 2: b (10 syllables)
Line 3: a (10 syllables)
Line 4: b (10 syllables)
Line 5: b (10 syllables)
Line 6: c (10 syllables)
Line 7: b (10 syllables)
Line 8: c (10 syllables)
Line 9: c (12 syllables - alexandrine)
```

## Historical Context
- **Creator**: Edmund Spenser (1552-1599)
- **First Use**: *The Faerie Queene* (1590-1596)
- **Purpose**: Created to suit the needs of his allegorical epic
- **Innovation**: Unique combination of existing metrical elements

## Literary Characteristics
- **Interlocking Rhyme**: The rhyme scheme creates musical unity
- **Alexandrine Conclusion**: The final longer line provides closure and emphasis
- **Narrative Suitability**: Well-suited for extended narrative poetry
- **Musical Quality**: Creates a sense of completion and harmony
- **Flexibility**: Allows for both narrative progression and lyrical moments

## Usage and Influence
- **Primary Work**: *The Faerie Queene* by Edmund Spenser
- **Later Adoptions**: Used by later poets including:
  - Lord Byron (*Childe Harold's Pilgrimage*)
  - John Keats (*The Eve of St. Agnes*)
  - Percy Bysshe Shelley (*Adonais*)

## Literary Significance
- **Artistic Achievement**: Demonstrates Spenser's technical mastery
- **Narrative Function**: Provides structure for complex allegorical content
- **Musical Quality**: Creates a distinctive sound pattern
- **Influence**: Inspired later poets to experiment with stanza forms
- **English Epic**: Helped establish a distinctly English form for epic poetry
