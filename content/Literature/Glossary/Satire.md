# Satire

**Satire** is a literary and artistic genre that uses humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize foolishness, corruption, or vice in individuals, institutions, or society as a whole. The primary purpose of satire is often to inspire social or political change, though it can also be used for purely comedic effect.

## Types of Satire

There are two main types of satire, named after the Roman satirists who pioneered them:

*   **Horatian Satire**: This type of satire is gentle, light-hearted, and witty. It aims to amuse and entertain rather than to shock or offend, and it often uses self-deprecation and a playful tone to poke fun at human folly. The goal of Horatian satire is to correct vice or foolishness with a smile.
*   **Juvenalian Satire**: This type of satire is more bitter, angry, and contemptuous. It uses irony, sarcasm, and moral indignation to attack what it sees as serious social or political problems. The goal of Juvenalian satire is to provoke a strong emotional reaction in the reader and to inspire them to take action.

## Examples

*   **Horatian Satire**: Alexander Pope's "The Rape of the Lock" is a classic example of Horatian satire. It gently mocks the vanity and triviality of high society in 18th-century England.
*   **Juvenalian Satire**: Jonathan Swift's "A Modest Proposal" is a famous example of Juvenalian satire. It uses savage irony to criticize the British government's treatment of the Irish people.
