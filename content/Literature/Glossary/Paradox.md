# Paradox

## Definition
A **Paradox** is a literary device that presents a statement or situation that appears contradictory or absurd but reveals a deeper truth upon closer examination. It challenges conventional thinking by combining seemingly opposite ideas.

## Types
- **Logical Paradox**: Statement that contradicts itself logically
- **Literary Paradox**: Apparent contradiction that reveals truth
- **Situational Paradox**: Circumstances that seem contradictory
- **Verbal Paradox**: Words that seem to contradict but don't

## Examples
- "Less is more"
- "The only constant is change"
- Donne's "Death Be Not Proud": Death itself shall die
- "I must be cruel only to be kind" (Hamlet)

## Function
- **Provoke Thought**: Forces readers to think deeper
- **Reveal Truth**: Uncovers hidden meanings
- **Create Tension**: Generates intellectual conflict
- **Emphasize Ideas**: Makes concepts memorable

## Literary Use
- **Metaphysical Poetry**: Donne's religious and love poems
- **Shakespeare**: Throughout plays and sonnets
- **Religious Writing**: Exploring divine mysteries
- **Philosophical Literature**: Examining life's contradictions
