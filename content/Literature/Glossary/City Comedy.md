## Definition
**City Comedy** is a satirical dramatic genre that emerged in the Jacobean period, focusing on the lives, vices, and follies of the urban middle class in London. These comedies used wit and humor to expose the moral corruption and social pretensions of city dwellers.

## Key Characteristics
- **Urban Setting**: Set in contemporary London
- **Middle-Class Focus**: Characters from merchant and professional classes
- **Satirical Tone**: Mocks social vices and pretensions
- **Realistic Dialogue**: Natural speech patterns of the time
- **Moral Corruption**: Exposes greed, lust, and social climbing

## Major Practitioners
- **Ben Jonson**: *Volpone*, *The Alchemist*
- **Thomas Middleton**: *A Chaste Maid in Cheapside*
- **Thomas Dekker**: *The Shoemaker's Holiday*

## Purpose
- Critique contemporary urban society
- Expose moral and social corruption
- Entertain through satirical observation
- Reflect changing social dynamics
