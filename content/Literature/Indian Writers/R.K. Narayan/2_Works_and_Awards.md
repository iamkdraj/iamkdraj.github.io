# 2. Works and Awards of R.K. Narayan

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of R.K. Narayan's major works and the significant awards and honors he received for his contributions to literature.

## Major Works

Narayan was a prolific writer, known primarily for his novels and short stories set in the fictional South Indian town of Malgudi.

### Novels

Narayan's novels often explore the lives of ordinary people, their struggles, and their aspirations, with a characteristic blend of humor and gentle irony.

*   **_Swami and Friends_** (1935) - His debut novel, introducing the fictional town of Malgudi.
*   **_The Bachelor of Arts_** (1937) - Part of his semi-autobiographical trilogy, following the growth of Chandran.
*   **_The Dark Room_** (1938) - Focuses on the plight of a submissive housewife, Savitri.
*   **_The English Teacher_** (1945) - The final part of his semi-autobiographical trilogy, telling the story of Krishna.
*   **_Mr. Sampath: The Printer of Malgudi_** (1949) - Later retitled *Mr. Sampath: The Printer of Malgudi*.
*   **_The Financial Expert_** (1952) - Considered by some to be his masterpiece, featuring Margayya.
*   **_Waiting for the Mahatma_** (1955) - Notable for featuring Mahatma Gandhi as a character.
*   **_The Guide_** (1958) - His most famous novel, winning the Sahitya Akademi Award, featuring Raju, Rosie, and Marco.
*   **_The Man-Eater of Malgudi_** (1961)
*   **_The Vendor of Sweets_** (1967) - The story of Jagan and his son Mali.
*   **_A Tiger for Malgudi_** (1983)
*   **_Talkative Man_** (1986)
*   **_The World of Nagaraj_** (1990)
*   **_Grandmother's Tale_** (1992)

### Short Story Collections

Narayan's short stories are celebrated for their conciseness, wit, and keen observation of human nature.

*   **_Malgudi Days_** (1942) - A collection of stories set in Malgudi.
*   **_An Astrologer's Day and Other Stories_** (1947)
*   **_Lawley Road and Other Stories_** (1956)
*   **_A Horse and Two Goats_** (1970)
*   **_Under the Banyan Tree and Other Stories_** (1985)
*   **_The Grandmother's Tale and Selected Stories_** (1994)

### Non-Fiction

*   **_Next Sunday_** (1960)
*   **_My Dateless Diary_** (1960) - A travelogue of his visit to the USA.
*   **_My Days_** (1974) - His autobiography.
*   **_Reluctant Guru_** (1974)
*   **_The Emerald Route_** (1980)
*   **_A Writer's Nightmare_** (1988)
*   **_A Story-Teller's World_** (1989)
*   **_The Writerly Life_** (2001)

### Mythology

Narayan also retold Indian epics and myths in his characteristic simple and engaging style.

*   **_Gods, Demons and Others_** (1964)
*   **_The Ramayana_** (1973)
*   **_The Mahabharata_** (1978)

## Awards and Honors

*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (1960):** For his novel *The Guide*.
*   **Padma Bhushan (1964):** India's third-highest civilian award.
*   **Filmfare Award (1980):** For the film adaptation of *The Guide*.
*   **Padma Vibhushan (2000):** India's second-highest civilian award.
*   **Nominated for Nobel Prize in Literature:** Several times.
*   **Nominated to Rajya Sabha:** The upper house of the Indian Parliament.
