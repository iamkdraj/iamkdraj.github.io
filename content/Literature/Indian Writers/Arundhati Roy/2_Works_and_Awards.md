This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Arundhati Roy's major works across fiction and non-fiction, and the significant awards and honors she has received for her contributions to literature and activism.

## Major Works

Arundhati Roy is known for her powerful fiction and her extensive body of non-fiction work, which often addresses political and social issues.

### Fiction

*   **_The God of Small Things_** (1997) - Her debut novel, which won the Man Booker Prize. A semi-autobiographical story set in Kerala, exploring themes of love, caste, and family secrets.
*   **_The Ministry of Utmost Happiness_** (2017) - Her second novel, published after a 20-year hiatus from fiction. It takes readers on a journey from Old Delhi to Kashmir, exploring themes of identity, love, and social justice.

### Non-Fiction (Essays and Speeches)

Roy's non-fiction works are often critical commentaries on globalization, neo-liberalism, nuclear weapons, and human rights issues.

*   **_The End of Imagination_** (1998) - An essay collection.
*   **_The Cost of Living_** (1999) - Critiques large-scale development projects like dams and the impact of nuclear weapons.
*   **_The Greater Common Good_** (1999) - Focuses on the Narmada Valley Project.
*   **_The Algebra of Infinite Justice_** (2002) - A collection of essays for which she won (but declined) the Sahitya Akademi Award.
*   **_Power Politics_** (2001)
*   **_War Talk_** (2003)
*   **_An Ordinary Person's Guide to Empire_** (2004)
*   **_Public Power in the Age of Empire_** (2004)
*   **_The Checkbook and the Cruise Missile_** (2004)
*   **_The Shape of the Beast_** (2008)
*   **_Listening to Grasshoppers: Field Notes on Democracy_** (2010)
*   **_Broken Republic_** (2011)
*   **_Walking with the Comrades_** (2011) - Chronicles her journey with Maoist guerrillas.
*   **_Kashmir: The Case for Freedom_** (2011)
*   **_The Hanging of Afzal Guru and the Strange Case of the Attack on the Indian Parliament_** (2013)
*   **_Capitalism: A Ghost Story_** (2014)
*   **_Things That Can and Cannot Be Said_** (2016) - With John Cusack.
*   **_My Seditious Heart_** (2019) - A comprehensive collection of her non-fiction essays.

### Screenplays

*   **_In Which Annie Gives It Those Ones_** (1989) - Won a National Film Award.
*   **_Electric Moon_** (1992)

## Awards and Honors

*   **Man Booker Prize for Fiction (1997):** For her debut novel *The God of Small Things*.
*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (2004):** For *The Algebra of Infinite Justice* (declined).
*   **Lannan Cultural Freedom Award (2002)**
*   **Sydney Peace Prize (2004)**
*   **Norman Mailer Prize for Distinguished Writing (2011)**
