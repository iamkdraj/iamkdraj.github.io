# 2. Works and Awards of Jayant Mahapatra

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Jayant Mahapatra's major works in both English and Oriya, and the significant awards and honors he received for his contributions to poetry.

## Major Works

Jayant Mahapatra was a prolific poet, writing extensively in both English and Oriya. His works are known for their meditative quality, subtle imagery, and deep engagement with the cultural and spiritual heritage of Odisha.

### English Poetry Collections

*   **_Close the Sky, Ten by Ten_** (1971) - His debut volume.
*   **_Svayamvara and Other Poems_** (1971)
*   **_A Father's House_** (1976)
*   **_A Rain of Rites_** (1976) - Includes the famous poem "Dawn at Puri."
*   **_Waiting_** (1979) - Explores the poet's relationship with the tradition and culture of Orissa.
*   **_The False Start_** (1980)
*   **_Relationship_** (1980) - His long poem that won him the Sahitya Akademi Award.
*   **_Life Signs_** (1983) - Deals with hunger and famine.
*   **_Dispossessed Nests_** (1986)
*   **_Burden of Waves and Fruit_** (1988)
*   **_Temple_** (1989)
*   **_A Whiteness of Bones_** (1992) - Considered one of his best collections.
*   **_Shadow Space_** (1997)
*   **_Bare Face_** (2000)
*   **_Random Descent_** (2006)
*   **_Land_** (2013)

### Oriya Poetry Collections

*   He authored 7 books of poems in Oriya.

## Awards and Honors

*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (1981):** For his long poem *Relationship*. He was the first Indian poet to win this award for English poetry.
*   **Padma Shri (2009):** India's fourth-highest civilian award. He returned this award in 2015 in protest against "growing intolerance" in India.
*   **Jacob Glatstein Memorial Award:** Conferred by *Poetry* magazine, Chicago.
*   **Allen Tate Poetry Prize (2009):** From *The Sewanee Review*.
*   **SAARC Literary Award (2009)**
