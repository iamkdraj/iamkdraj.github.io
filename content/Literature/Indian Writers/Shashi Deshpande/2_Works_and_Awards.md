# 2. Works and Awards of Shashi Deshpande

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Shashi Deshpande's major works across various genres and the significant awards and honors she has received for her contributions to literature.

## Major Works

Shashi Deshpande is primarily known for her novels and short stories, which often focus on the inner lives and struggles of Indian women.

### Novels

*   **_The Dark Holds No Terrors_** (1980) - Her first novel, exploring the life of Saru (Sarita), an educated doctor grappling with a troubled marriage and past.
*   **_If I Die Today_** (1982) - A mystery thriller.
*   **_Come Up and Be Dead_** (1983) - Another mystery thriller.
*   **_Roots and Shadows_** (1983) - The story of Indu, an educated and sensitive girl.
*   **_That Long Silence_** (1988) - Won the Sahitya Akademi Award. Explores the life of Jaya and her husband Mohan, and the protagonist's quest for self-identity.
*   **_The Binding Vine_** (1992) - Deals with themes of marital rape, violence against women, and the search for truth.
*   **_A Matter of Time_** (1996) - Explores intricate relationships within an extended family across three generations.
*   **_Small Remedies_** (2000) - The story of Madhu, a woman coping with personal tragedy and seeking solace.
*   **_Moving On_** (2004) - Explores themes of widowhood and finding a new life.
*   **_In the Country of Deceit_** (2008) - Features Devayani Mudhol, a young woman navigating complex relationships.
*   **_Shadow Play_** (2013)
*   **_The Narayanpur Incident_** (A novel based on the Quit India Movement and the role of children in it).

### Short Story Collections

*   **_The Legacy_** (1978) - Her first collection of short stories.
*   **_It Was the Nightingale_** (1986)
*   **_The Intrusion and Other Stories_** (1993) - Includes the controversial story "The Intrusion" dealing with marital rape.
*   **_The Stone Women_** (2000)

### Children's Books

*   **_A Summer Adventure_**
*   **_The Hidden Treasure_**
*   **_The Only Witness_**
*   **_The Narayanpur Incident_** (Children's version of the novel)

## Awards and Honors

*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (1990):** For her novel *That Long Silence*.
*   **Padma Shri (2009):** India's fourth-highest civilian award, recognizing her contributions to literature.
