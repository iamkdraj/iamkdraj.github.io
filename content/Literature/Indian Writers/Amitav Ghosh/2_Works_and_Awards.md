# 2. Works and Awards of Amitav Ghosh

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Amitav Ghosh's major works across various genres and the significant awards and honors he has received for his contributions to literature.

## Major Works

Amitav Ghosh is primarily known for his historical fiction, but he has also written significant non-fiction works and essays.

### Novels

*   **_The Circle of Reason_** (1986) - His debut novel, telling the story of Nachiketa or Alu.
*   **_The Shadow Lines_** (1988) - Won the Sahitya Akademi Award. Explores themes of memory, identity, and the partition of Bengal.
*   **_The Calcutta Chromosome_** (1995) - Won the Arthur C. Clarke Award. A medical thriller based on the life of Sir Ronald Ross.
*   **_The Glass Palace_** (2000) - A historical novel spanning Burma, Malaya, and India, focusing on the Burmese royal family and an orphaned Indian boy, Rajkumar.
*   **_The Hungry Tide_** (2004) - Set in the Sundarbans, exploring environmental and human themes.
*   **_Sea of Poppies_** (2008) - First book in the Ibis Trilogy, shortlisted for the Booker Prize.
*   **_River of Smoke_** (2011) - Second book in the Ibis Trilogy.
*   **_Flood of Fire_** (2015) - Third and final book in the Ibis Trilogy.
*   **_Gun Island_** (2019) - Deals with climate change and human migration.

### Non-Fiction

*   **_In an Antique Land_** (1992) - A blend of travelogue, history, and anthropology.
*   **_Dancing in Cambodia and At Large in Burma_** (1998) - An anthology of essays.
*   **_Countdown_** (1999) - Explores India's nuclear tests.
*   **_The Imam and the Indian_** (2002) - A collection of essays on different themes.
*   **_The Great Derangement: Climate Change and the Unthinkable_** (2016) - Critiques literature's failure to address climate change.
*   **_Jungle Nama_** (2021) - A graphic verse novel, a retelling of a Bengali folk tale.
*   **_Smoke and Ashes: A Writer's Journey Through Opium's Hidden Histories_** (2023) - Explores the history of opium and its connection to climate change.

## Awards and Honors

*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (1989):** For his novel *The Shadow Lines*.
*   **Arthur C. Clarke Award (1997):** For his novel *The Calcutta Chromosome*.
*   **54th Jnanpith Award (2018):** India's highest literary honor, making him the first English-language writer to receive it.
*   **Padma Shri (2007):** India's fourth-highest civilian award.
*   **Booker Prize Shortlist:** *Sea of Poppies* (2008).
*   **Commonwealth Writers' Prize:** He withdrew *The Glass Palace* from consideration for this award.
