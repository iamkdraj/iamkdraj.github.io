This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Mulk Raj Anand's major works and the significant awards and honors he received for his contributions to literature and art.

## Major Works

Anand was a prolific writer, known primarily for his novels and short stories that championed the cause of the underprivileged.

### Novels

Anand's novels are powerful social documents that critique the injustices of the caste system and economic exploitation.

*   **_Untouchable_** (1935) - His debut and most famous protest novel.
*   **_Coolie_** (1936) - A prose epic depicting the life of a young boy, Munoo.
*   **_Two Leaves and a Bud_** (1937) - Exposes the miserable plight of tea plantation laborers.
*   **_The Village_** (1939) - The first novel in the Lal Singh trilogy.
*   **_Across the Black Waters_** (1939) - The second novel in the trilogy, detailing Lal Singh's experiences in WWI.
*   **_The Sword and the Sickle_** (1942) - The final novel in the trilogy.
*   **_The Big Heart_** (1945) - Focuses on the conflict between hereditary coppersmiths and capitalists.
*   **_The Private Life of an Indian Prince_** (1953) - A psychological novel about the decline of a Maharaja.
*   **_The Old Woman and the Cow_** (1960) - Notable for being his only novel with a female protagonist, Gauri.
*   **_The Road_** (1961) - Revisits the theme of untouchability.
*   **_The Death of a Hero_** (1964)

### Autobiographical Novels

Anand wrote a seven-volume autobiographical series titled *Seven Ages of Man*, of which he completed four.

*   **_Seven Summers_** (1951) - The first volume, a memoir of his childhood.
*   **_Morning Face_** (1968) - The second volume, which won the Sahitya Akademi Award.
*   **_Confession of a Lover_** (1976)
*   **_The Bubble_** (1984)

### Short Story Collections

*   **_The Lost Child and Other Stories_** (1934)
*   **_The Barber's Trade Union and Other Stories_** (1944)
*   **_The Tractor and the Corn Goddess and Other Stories_** (1947)
*   **_Reflections on the Golden Bed and Other Stories_** (1953)
*   **_The Power of Darkness and Other Stories_** (1959)
*   **_Lajwanti and Other Stories_** (1966)
*   **_Between Tears and Laughter_** (1973)

### Other Works

*   **_Kama Kala_** (1958) - A work on Indian art.
*   **_Homage to Khajuraho_**
*   **_Conversations in Bloomsbury_** (1981) - A memoir of his time in London.
*   **_Marg_** - The influential arts magazine he founded and edited.

## Awards and Honors

*   **International Peace Prize (1953):** Awarded by the World Peace Council.
*   **Padma Bhushan (1968):** India's third-highest civilian award.
*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (1971):** Awarded for his autobiographical novel *Morning Face*.
*   **Leverhulme Fellowship**
