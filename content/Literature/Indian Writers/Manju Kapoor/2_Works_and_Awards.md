# 2. Works and Awards of Manju Kapur

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Manju Kapur's major works and the significant awards and honors she has received for her contributions to literature.

## Major Works

Manju Kapur is primarily known for her novels, which explore themes of family, tradition, marriage, and the struggles of women in contemporary India.

### Novels

*   **_Difficult Daughters_** (1998) - Her debut novel, which won the Commonwealth Writers' Prize for Best First Book (Eurasia region). It tells the story of Virmati, a woman torn between family duty and personal ambition, set against the backdrop of the Partition of India.
*   **_A Married Woman_** (2003) - Explores the inner turmoil of its protagonist, Astha, and her journey of self-discovery amidst marital and societal expectations. This novel was adapted into a web series.
*   **_Home_** (2006) - Delves into the dynamics of a joint family and the challenges faced by women within its structure.
*   **_The Immigrant_** (2008) - A compelling portrait of an arranged marriage and the experiences of Indian immigrants in Canada. This novel was adapted into the TV series *Pardes Mein Hai Mera Dil*.
*   **_Custody_** (2011) - Explores the complexities of divorce and child custody battles in India. This novel was adapted into the popular Hindi TV series *Yeh Hai Mohabbatein*.
*   **_Brothers_** (2016) - Focuses on sibling relationships and family secrets.

### Non-Fiction

*   **_Shaping the World: Women Writers on Themselves_** (2014) - A collection of essays.

## Awards and Honors

*   **Commonwealth Writers' Prize for Best First Book (Eurasia region) (1999):** For her debut novel *Difficult Daughters*.
