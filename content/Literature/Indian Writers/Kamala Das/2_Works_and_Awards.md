# 2. Works and Awards of Kamala Das

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Kamala Das's major works in both English and Malayalam, and the significant awards and honors she received for her groundbreaking contributions to literature.

## Major Works

Kamala Das wrote extensively in both English and Malayalam (under the pen name Madhavi Kutty). Her works are known for their confessional style and fearless exploration of themes related to female identity and sexuality.

### English Poetry Collections

*   **_Summer in Calcutta_** (1965) - Her first and most famous English poetry collection. Includes "The Testing of Sirens," "The Dance of Eunuchs," and "The Looking Glass."
*   **_The Descendants_** (1967)
*   **_The Old Playhouse and Other Poems_** (1973)
*   **_Stranger Time_** (1977)
*   **_Collected Poems_** (1984) - Won the Sahitya Akademi Award.
*   **_Only the Soul Knows How to Sing_** (1997)
*   **_The Anamalai Poems_**
*   **_Tonight, This Savage Rite_** (1979) - A collaborative work with Pritish Nandy.

### Selected Individual Poems

*   "My Grandmother's House" - A highly autobiographical and poignant poem.
*   "An Introduction" - A powerful statement on identity and self-assertion.
*   "The Dance of the Eunuchs"
*   "The Looking Glass"
*   "A Hot Noon in Malabar"
*   "My Mother at Sixty-Six"

### Novels

*   **_Alphabet of Lust_** (1976)
*   **_A Doll for the Child Prostitute_** (1977)

### Autobiography

*   **_My Story_** (1976) - Originally published in Malayalam as *Ente Katha* (1973). A controversial and groundbreaking autobiography.

### Short Story Collections (Malayalam)

*   **_Pakshiyude Manam_** (The Scent of the Bird) (1964)
*   **_Thanuppu_** (Cold) (1967) - Won the Kerala State Sahitya Akademi Award.

## Awards and Honors

*   **Kerala State Sahitya Akademi Award (1969):** For her short story collection *Thanuppu*.
*   **Sahitya Akademi Award (1985):** For her *Collected Poems*.
*   **Asian World Prize for Literature (1985)**
*   **Nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature (1984)**
*   **"The Mother of Modern Indian English Poetry":** A title earned for her groundbreaking contributions.
