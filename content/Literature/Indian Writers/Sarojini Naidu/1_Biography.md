# 1. Biography of Sarojini Naidu

**(13 February 1879 – 2 March 1949)**

## Introduction

Sarojini Naidu, often hailed as **"The Nightingale of India" (Bharat Kokila)** by Mahatma Gandhi, was a remarkable figure who seamlessly blended her poetic genius with fervent political activism. She was a leading Indo-Anglian poet and a pivotal figure in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule. A staunch proponent of civil rights, women's emancipation, and anti-imperialistic ideals, her life and work left an indelible mark on both Indian literature and politics.

## Early Life and Education

Sarojini Naidu was born on February 13, 1879, in Hyderabad, into a Bengali Hindu family. Her father, Aghorenath Chattopadhyay, was a scientist and philosopher, and her mother, Barada Sundari Devi, was a poet. This intellectual and artistic environment fostered her early talents.

She received her early education in Madras (now Chennai). At the young age of 12, she wrote her first play, *Maher Muneer*, which gained her recognition and impressed the Nawab of Hyderabad. At 16, she received a scholarship from the Nizam of Hyderabad, which enabled her to travel to England. She studied at King's College London and later at Girton College, Cambridge.

During her time in England, she met prominent literary figures like **Arthur Symons** and **Edmund Gosse**. It was Gosse who advised her to focus on Indian themes for her poetry, a suggestion that profoundly shaped her literary direction.

## Literary Career

Naidu's poetry is characterized by its rich sensory imagery, lyrical quality, and vivid depictions of Indian life, culture, and nature. She wrote primarily in English, often employing the form of lyric poetry in the tradition of British Romanticism. Her work earned her comparisons to poets like Keats and Yeats, with some calling her the "Indian Keats" or "Indian Yeats."

Her first book of poems, **_The Golden Threshold_** (1905), was published in London with an introduction by Arthur Symons. This was followed by **_The Bird of Time: Songs of Life, Death & the Spring_** (1912) and **_The Broken Wing_** (1917), the last collection published during her lifetime. Her poems often explored themes of patriotism, romance, tragedy, and the everyday lives of Indian people.

## Political Activism

Naidu's literary career soon intertwined with her deep commitment to India's freedom struggle. She became an active participant in the nationalist movement, working closely with Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, and other prominent leaders. She was a powerful orator and traveled extensively across India, advocating for women's rights, Hindu-Muslim unity, and civil disobedience.

Her political milestones include:

*   **President of the Indian National Congress (1925):** She was the first Indian woman to hold this prestigious position.
*   **Governor of the United Provinces (1947):** After India gained independence, she became the first woman to hold the office of Governor in the Dominion of India.

She was imprisoned multiple times for her involvement in the nationalist cause, always marching in Mahatma Gandhi's footsteps.

## Later Life and Legacy

Sarojini Naidu passed away on March 2, 1949, in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, due to a cardiac arrest. Her legacy is multifaceted. As a poet, she is remembered for her beautiful and evocative verse that captured the spirit of India. As a political leader, she is celebrated for her courage, eloquence, and unwavering dedication to the cause of Indian independence and women's empowerment. Her life serves as an inspiration for her ability to combine artistic expression with a profound commitment to social and political change.
