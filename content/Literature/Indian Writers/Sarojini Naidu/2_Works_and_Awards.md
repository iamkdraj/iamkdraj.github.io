# 2. Works and Awards of Sarojini Naidu

This section provides a comprehensive catalog of Sarojini Naidu's major works and the significant honors she received for her contributions to poetry and public life.

## Major Works

Sarojini Naidu's poetic output is characterized by its lyrical quality, rich imagery, and focus on Indian themes. She wrote primarily in English.

### Poetry Collections

*   **_The Golden Threshold_** (1905) - Her first book of poems, published in London with an introduction by Arthur Symons. It contains 40 poems classified into three sections: folk poems, songs for music, and other poems. Notable poems include "Palanquin Bearers," "Wandering Singers," and "Indian Weavers."

*   **_The Bird of Time: Songs of Life, Death & the Spring_** (1912) - Her second collection, with an introduction by Edmund Gosse. It contains 46 poems divided into four sections. Notable poems include "The Dirge" and "An Indian Love Song."

*   **_The Broken Wing: Songs of Love, Death and Destiny including 'The Gift of India'_** (1917) - The last of her books published in her lifetime, containing 61 lyrics. It includes "Songs of Life and Death" and "Lotus" (a sonnet dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi).

*   **_The Sceptred Flute: Songs of India_** (1943) - A posthumously published collection that compiles poems from her first three volumes.

*   **_The Feather of the Dawn_** (1961) - Another posthumous collection, published by her daughter Padmaja. It contains 37 poems (5 sonnets and 32 short lyrics).

## Awards and Honors

Sarojini Naidu's contributions were recognized both for her literary talent and her political leadership.

*   **"The Nightingale of India" (Bharat Kokila):** A sobriquet given to her by Mahatma Gandhi for the lyrical quality of her poetry.
*   **President of the Indian National Congress (1925):** A significant political achievement, making her the first Indian woman to hold this position.
*   **Governor of the United Provinces (1947):** The first woman to hold a gubernatorial office in the Dominion of India.
