This section provides a catalog of Rabindranath Tagore's major works across various genres and the significant awards and honors he received. 

## Major Works

Tagore was a prolific writer whose works have been translated into numerous languages. Below are some of his most significant contributions, organized by genre.

### Poetry Collections

Tagore wrote nearly fifty volumes of poetry. His work is celebrated for its lyrical quality, spiritual depth, and rhythmic beauty.

*   **_Sandhya Sangeet_** (Evening Songs) - 1882
*   **_Prabhat Sangeet_** (Morning Songs) - 1883
*   **_Mansi_** (The Ideal One) - 1890
*   **_Sonar Tari_** (The Golden Boat) - 1894
*   **_Gitanjali_** (Song Offerings) - 1910 (Bengali), 1912 (English)
*   **_Gitimalya_** (Wreath of Songs) - 1914
*   **_Balaka_** (The Flight of Cranes) - 1916
*   **_The Gardener_** - 1913 (A collection of love lyrics)
*   **_The Crescent Moon_**
*   **_The Fugitive_**
*   **_The Child_** (His only major poem written directly in English)
*   **_Utsarga_** (Offering)

### Novels

Tagore's novels often explored complex social and political issues of his time.

*   **_Chokher Bali_** (A Grain of Sand) - 1903
*   **_The Wreck_** - 1906 (Bengali), 1921 (English)
*   **_Gora_** (Fair-Faced) - 1910 (Considered by many to be his most ambitious novel)
*   **_Ghare-Baire_** (The Home and the World) - 1916 (Bengali), 1919 (English)
*   **_Yogayog_** (Crosscurrents) - 1929
*   **_Shesher Kabita_** (The Last Poem) - 1929
*   **_Hungry Stones_**
*   **_Broken Ties_**

### Plays

Tagore wrote over fifty plays and is considered a father of modern Indian stagecraft.

*   **_Valmiki Pratibha_** (The Genius of Valmiki) - 1881
*   **_Raja_** (The King of the Dark Chamber) - 1910
*   **_Dak Ghar_** (The Post Office) - 1912
*   **_Chitra_** - 1913
*   **_Sacrifice_**
*   **_Malini_**
*   **_Red Oleanders_**
*   **_Chandalika_** (The Untouchable Girl) - 1933
*   **_The Cycle of Spring_**
*   **_Natir Puja_** (The Dancing Girl's Worship)

### Short Story Collections / Famous Stories

*   **_Galpaguchchha_** (A Bunch of Stories) - A collection of his short stories.
*   **"The Kabuliwala"** (The Fruitseller from Kabul)
*   **"The Postmaster"**
*   **"The Homecoming"**

### Non-Fiction and Essays

*   **_Jivansmriti_** (My Reminiscences) - 1912
*   **_Chhelebela_** (My Boyhood Days) - 1940 (Autobiography)
*   **_Sadhana: The Realisation of Life_** (A collection of his lectures delivered in America)
*   **_The Cult of the Charka_** (An essay outlining his differences with Gandhi's Swadeshi movement)

## Awards and Recognition

*   **Nobel Prize in Literature (1913):** Awarded for the "profoundly sensitive, fresh and beautiful verse" of the English translation of *Gitanjali*.
*   **Knighthood (1915):** Conferred by King George V. Tagore renounced this honor in 1919 in protest of the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre.

## Other Notable Contributions

*   **National Anthems:** He composed the music and lyrics for the national anthems of **India** ("Jana Gana Mana") and **Bangladesh** ("Amar Sonar Bangla"). He also inspired the national anthem of **Sri Lanka**.
*   **Visva-Bharati University:** He founded the experimental school **Shantiniketan** in 1901, which later became Visva-Bharati University in 1921, a unique institution aimed at blending Eastern and Western educational philosophies.
