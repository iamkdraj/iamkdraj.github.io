## 🧩 Study Method Ideas

### 🔁 1. **Use “3-R” Chunking** for Every Topic:

**1 Read → 1 Recite → 1 Recall**

- Read a micro-topic (e.g., “Kohlberg’s stages”)
    
- Recite it aloud or write a summary from memory
    
- Recall later with flashcard or mini-test
    

### 🎲 2. **Turn Micro-Topics into Flashcards**

Use tools like:

- **Obsidian (with Toggle Cards)** ✅
    
- Anki (best if you're used to SRS)
    
- Manual paper flashcards if tech is distracting
    

Use the expanded headings as card prompts:

```
Q: What are the stages in Piaget’s theory of cognitive development?
Q: Define maladjustment and list its causes.
```

### 🧠 3. **Rapid Active Recall Sheets**

Make 1-page blank templates for:

- Personality Theories
    
- Learning Theories
    
- Developmental Stages
    
- Motivation Theories  
    Try to fill from memory every 2 days.
    

### 📋 4. **Convert PYQs into Categorized MCQ Sets**

- Map each PYQ to the expanded syllabus
    
- Tag as: “Easy / Confusing / Important”
    
- Re-attempt confusing ones after every revision
    

### 🗺️ 5. **Mindmap Complex Theories**

Use color-coded diagrams or even quick hand-drawn maps for:

- Learning Theories
    
- Motivation Theories
    
- Developmental stages
    
- Intelligence Theories
    

Stick them near your workspace for passive review.

### 🎙️ 6. **Oral Recap (Feynman Style)**

- Explain a topic aloud like you're teaching it
    
- Record yourself and play back
    
- Spot gaps and re-read that area
    

Works great for:

- Differences (Growth vs Development, Intrinsic vs Extrinsic)
    
- Definitions
    
- Process-based topics
    

### 📺 7. **Watch & Retain with Purpose**

Use short, exam-focused YouTube videos for:

- Constructivism
    
- Emotional Intelligence
    
- Personality Theories  
    But always **summarize or explain back** what you watched.
    

### 📈 8. **Mini Mock Exams**

Every 2–3 days:

- Do a 20-question MCQ set from 2–3 chapters
    
- Analyze wrong answers and revise the concept
    
- Track weak zones in a spreadsheet
