# Child Development

## I. Introduction
- Definition: Study of how children grow and change over time.
- Key Areas: Physical, Cognitive, Social-Emotional.
- Influences: Nature (genetics) vs. Nurture (environment).

## II. Developmental Stages

### A. Prenatal Development (Conception to Birth)
- Germinal Stage (0-2 weeks): Fertilization, implantation.
- Embryonic Stage (3-8 weeks): Organ formation, rapid growth.
- Fetal Stage (9 weeks-birth): Maturation of systems, significant growth.

### B. Infancy (Birth to 1 year)
- Physical: Rapid growth, motor skill development (rolling, sitting, crawling, walking).
- Cognitive: Sensorimotor stage (Piaget), object permanence, basic problem-solving.
- Social-Emotional: Attachment (Bowlby), trust vs. mistrust (Erikson), stranger anxiety.

### C. Toddlerhood (1-3 years)
- Physical: Refined motor skills, toilet training.
- Cognitive: Preoperational thought (Piaget), language explosion, symbolic play.
- Social-Emotional: Autonomy vs. shame/doubt (Erikson), temper tantrums, parallel play.

### D. Preschool (3-5 years)
- Physical: Improved coordination, fine motor skills.
- Cognitive: Egocentrism, magical thinking, curiosity, early literacy/numeracy.
- Social-Emotional: Initiative vs. guilt (Erikson), cooperative play, developing friendships.

### E. Middle Childhood (6-11 years)
- Physical: Slower, steady growth, improved strength/coordination.
- Cognitive: Concrete operational thought (Piaget), logical thinking, conservation, academic skills.
- Social-Emotional: Industry vs. inferiority (Erikson), self-concept, peer relationships, moral development.

### F. Adolescence (12-18 years)
- Physical: Puberty, rapid growth spurt.
- Cognitive: Formal operational thought (Piaget), abstract thinking, hypothetical reasoning, identity formation.
- Social-Emotional: Identity vs. role confusion (Erikson), peer influence, independence, romantic relationships.

## III. Key Theories of Child Development

### A. Psychodynamic Theories
- **Sigmund Freud (Psychosexual Development):** Oral, Anal, Phallic, Latency, Genital stages.
- **Erik Erikson (Psychosocial Development):** 8 stages, each with a crisis to resolve (e.g., Trust vs. Mistrust).

### B. Cognitive Theories
- **Jean Piaget (Cognitive Development):** Sensorimotor, Preoperational, Concrete Operational, Formal Operational stages.
- **Lev Vygotsky (Sociocultural Theory):** Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD), scaffolding, social interaction.

### C. Behavioral and Social Learning Theories
- **John B. Watson & B.F. Skinner (Behaviorism):** Classical and Operant Conditioning.
- **Albert Bandura (Social Learning Theory):** Observational learning, modeling, self-efficacy.

### D. Humanistic Theories
- **Abraham Maslow (Hierarchy of Needs):** Self-actualization.
- **Carl Rogers (Client-Centered Therapy):** Unconditional positive regard, self-concept.

### E. Ecological Systems Theory
- **Urie Bronfenbrenner:** Microsystem, Mesosystem, Exosystem, Macrosystem, Chronosystem.

## IV. Research Methods in Child Development
- Longitudinal Studies
- Cross-sectional Studies
- Sequential Studies
- Observation
- Experiments
- Surveys

## V. Importance of Studying Child Development
- Inform parenting practices.
- Guide educational strategies.
- Develop effective interventions.
- Understand societal issues related to children.
- Promote healthy development.
