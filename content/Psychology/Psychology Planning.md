- Lifespan development

- Development is continuous
- Development is predictable
- Individual differences in development
- Development is affected by maturation and learning

### 2.3 Stages of Development
- Infancy
- Early childhood
- Later childhood
- Adolescence
- Adulthood

### 2.4 Areas of Development

#### 2.4.1 Physical Development
- Motor skills
- Puberty and physical changes
- Health and nutrition effects

#### 2.4.2 Emotional Development
- Emotional expression
- Emotional regulation
- Role of family and peers in emotional development

#### 2.4.3 Cognitive Development
- Mental abilities: memory, attention, reasoning
- Piaget’s stages:
  - Sensorimotor
  - Preoperational
  - Concrete operational
  - Formal operational

  - Zone of Proximal Development
  - Scaffolding
  - More Knowledgeable Other

  - Pre-conventional
  - Conventional

- Socialization process
- Role of family, peers, school

## 3. Learning

### 3.1 Meaning and Nature
- Definitions of learning
- Characteristics (permanent, goal-oriented, etc.)
- Learning curve

### 3.2 Types of Learning
- Motor learning
- Verbal learning
- Concept learning
- Problem-solving
- Observational learning
- Rote vs meaningful learning

### 3.3 Theories of Learning

#### 3.3.1 Behaviorist Theories
- Pavlov's Classical Conditioning:
  - UCS, UCR, CS, CR
  - Application in classroom (e.g., phobias, rewards)
- Skinner’s Operant Conditioning:
  - Reinforcement: Positive & Negative
  - Punishment
  - Schedules of reinforcement
  - Behavior modification techniques

#### 3.3.2 Cognitive Theories
- Insight learning (Köhler’s chimpanzee experiment)
- Tolman’s sign learning
- Information processing model (encoding, storage, retrieval)

#### 3.3.3 Observational Learning (Bandura)
- Attention
- Retention
- Reproduction
- Motivation

#### 3.3.4 Constructivist Theories
- Bruner’s Discovery Learning
- Scaffolding in learning
- Role of prior knowledge and active participation

### 3.4 Transfer of Learning
- Definition and importance
- Types:
  - Positive
  - Negative
  - Zero
- Theories:
  - Identical Elements
  - Generalization
  - Mental discipline
- Factors affecting transfer

### 3.5 Factors Affecting Learning
- Internal: interest, motivation, attention, aptitude
- External: teaching methods, environment
- Teacher-related: style, personality, feedback

## 4. Personality

### 4.1 Meaning and Nature
- Definitions by Allport, Cattell, etc.
- Traits and behavior patterns
- Dynamic and holistic view

### 4.2 Determinants of Personality
- Heredity (genes, temperament)
- Environment (family, culture, education)
- Situational factors (peer influence, crisis)

### 4.3 Theories of Personality
- **Type Theories:**
  - Hippocrates’ 4 humors
  - Jung’s introvert vs extrovert
- **Trait Theories:**
  - Allport’s cardinal, central, secondary traits
  - Cattell’s 16 personality factors
  - Eysenck’s PEN model
- **Psychoanalytic Theory:**
  - Freud’s structure: Id, Ego, Superego
  - Defense mechanisms
- **Humanistic Theory:**
  - Maslow’s Self-Actualization
  - Rogers’ self-concept

### 4.4 Measurement of Personality
- Projective techniques: Rorschach, TAT
- Inventories: MMPI, Big Five
- Observational and rating scales

### 4.5 Adjustment and Maladjustment
- Meaning of adjustment
- Indicators of maladjustment
- Causes: family conflict, trauma, mental illness
- Coping strategies
- Role of school counselor

### 5.3 Emotional Intelligence

- Components: Self-awareness, Self-regulation, Motivation, Empathy, Social skills
- Classroom implications
- Comparison with IQ
