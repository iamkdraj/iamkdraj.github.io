## English Verb Tenses and Helping Verbs

*Note: The helping verbs `do`, `does`, and `did` are typically used to form questions and negative statements in the simple tenses.*

| Tense Type                     | Helping Verb(s)     | Main Verb Form  | Example                          |
| :----------------------------- | :------------------ | :-------------- | :------------------------------- |
| **Simple Present**             | do, does            | Base Form       | They **do** not *work* here.     |
| **Present Continuous**         | am, is, are         | -ing Form       | He **is** *working*.             |
| **Present Perfect**            | have, has           | Past Participle | We **have** *worked*.            |
| **Present Perfect Continuous** | have been, has been | -ing Form       | She **has been** *working*.      |
|                                |                     |                 |                                  |
| **Simple Past**                | did                 | Base Form       | I **did** *work* yesterday.      |
| **Past Continuous**            | was, were           | -ing Form       | They **were** *working*.         |
| **Past Perfect**               | had                 | Past Participle | He **had** *worked* there.       |
| **Past Perfect Continuous**    | had been            | -ing Form       | We **had been** *working*.       |
|                                |                     |                 |                                  |
| **Simple Future**              | will                | Base Form       | She **will** *work* tomorrow.    |
| **Future Continuous**          | will be             | -ing Form       | I **will be** *working*.         |
| **Future Perfect**             | will have           | Past Participle | They **will have** *worked*.     |
| **Future Perfect Continuous**  | will have been      | -ing Form       | He **will have been** *working*. |

## Verb Tense Correction Exercise

**Instructions:** Each sentence below contains an incorrect verb tense. Identify the mistake and rewrite the sentence using the correct form of the verb. Time clues are included to help you.

**Example:**
*   **Incorrect:** Yesterday, I **am seeing** a great movie.
*   **Correct:** Yesterday, I **saw** a great movie.

---

1.  Listen! I think someone **called** my name.
2.  By the time the police arrived, the thief **disappears**.
3.  I **am not visiting** the museum last weekend because I was sick.
4.  She **is working** in the garden for three hours and now she is taking a break.
5.  The earth **will revolve** around the sun.
6.  This time next month, I **relaxed** on a beach in Spain.
7.  Before he learned to cook, he **eats** takeout every single night.
8.  My brother **lives** in Canada since 2018.
9.  By 2030, scientists **will find** a cure for that disease.
10. We were watching a movie when the power **was going out**.

---

### Answer Key

1.  Listen! I think someone **is calling** my name. (The word "Listen!" indicates an action happening now - Present Continuous).
2.  By the time the police arrived, the thief **had disappeared**. (The thief's action happened before the police arrived - Past Perfect).
3.  I **did not visit** the museum last weekend because I was sick. (The phrase "last weekend" signals a completed past action - Simple Past).
4.  She **has been working** in the garden for three hours and now she is taking a break. (The action continued for a duration and just recently stopped - Present Perfect Continuous).
5.  The earth **revolves** around the sun. (This is a universal, timeless fact - Simple Present).
6.  This time next month, I **will be relaxing** on a beach in Spain. (This describes an action in progress at a specific time in the future - Future Continuous).
7.  Before he learned to cook, he **had eaten** takeout every single night. (This describes a past habit that occurred before another past action - Past Perfect).
8.  My brother **has lived** in Canada since 2018. (The action started in the past and continues to the present - Present Perfect).
9.  By 2030, scientists **will have found** a cure for that disease. (The action will be completed by a specific time in the future - Future Perfect).
10. We were watching a movie when the power **went out**. (A short, completed action that interrupted a longer, ongoing past action - Simple Past).
