# 📘 My Mother at Sixty-six – Kamala Das

---

## 🧾 Poem Summary

Kamala Das captures a poignant moment of realization—her mother is growing old. During a car ride to the airport, the poet is suddenly struck by her mother's pale, lifeless appearance. She experiences a wave of sorrow and fear, a feeling that takes her back to childhood fears of losing her. To distract herself, she looks out at lively scenes of youth. At the airport, before parting, she again sees her mother’s pale face and feels the ache of separation—but says nothing, choosing instead to hide her sadness behind a smile.

---

## 🧠 Line-by-Line Explanation

### **1–5:**
> Driving from my parent’s home to Cochin last Friday morning,  
> I saw my mother, beside me,  
> doze, open mouthed, her face ashen like that  
> of a corpse and realised with pain  
> that she was as old as she looked

- **Driving...Cochin**: The poet is traveling from her childhood home to the airport.
- **Dozing mother**: Mother is sleeping; vulnerable and tired.
- **Ashen like a corpse**: Pale and lifeless imagery.
- **Realised with pain**: Sudden emotional recognition of her mother's aging.

---

### **6–9:**
> but soon  
> put that thought away, and  
> looked out at Young  
> Trees sprinting, the merry children spilling out of their homes

- **Put that thought away**: She tries to suppress thoughts of death.
- **Trees sprinting**: Metaphor due to car movement—symbolizes life and motion.
- **Merry children**: Contrast with the mother; symbolizes vitality and innocence.

---

### **10–16:**
> but after the airport’s  
> security check, standing a few yards  
> away, I looked again at her, wan,  
> pale  
> as a late winter’s moon and felt that old  
> familiar ache, my childhood’s fear

- **Standing a few yards away**: The physical distance mirrors emotional distance.
- **Wan, pale as a late winter’s moon**: Weak, faded; symbol of aging and fragility.
- **Old familiar ache**: The poet has always feared losing her mother.
- **Childhood’s fear**: Deep-rooted fear of separation or death.

---

### **17–18:**
> but all I said was, see you soon, Amma,  
> all I did was smile and smile and smile...

- **See you soon**: Pretending normalcy.
- **Smile and smile and smile**: Repetition highlights the effort to hide emotion; it’s forced, masking pain.

---

## 🎯 Themes

### 1. **Aging and Mortality**
- The mother’s aging is central; the imagery emphasizes how close she is to death.
- The poet is forced to confront the reality of impermanence.

### 2. **Mother–Daughter Bond**
- The poem explores an intimate, emotional connection—silent but deep.
- Even in adulthood, the daughter remains emotionally vulnerable to her mother’s presence and aging.

### 3. **Fear of Separation**
- The daughter experiences the same fear she had as a child—losing her mother.
- The airport, a place of departures, symbolizes emotional and possibly permanent separation.

### 4. **Emotional Masking**
- The poet hides deep sorrow behind smiles.
- A commentary on how people manage pain in socially acceptable ways.

## 🧩 Symbolism

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Ashen face / corpse** | Imminent death, fragility of life |
| **Young trees & children** | Vibrancy, contrast to aging |
| **Late winter’s moon** | Fading life, loss of warmth and vitality |
| **Smile** | Emotional suppression, forced optimism |

---

## ✍️ Literary Devices

| Device | Example | Purpose |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Simile** | *ashen like that of a corpse*, *as a late winter’s moon* | Vivid imagery of aging |
| **Alliteration** | *smile and smile and smile* | Emphasizes repetition and emotional strain |
| **Enjambment** | Whole poem is one long sentence | Reflects unbroken, flowing emotions |
| **Imagery** | *trees sprinting*, *merry children* | Sharp contrast between youth and age |
| **Repetition** | *smile and smile and smile* | Conveys forced cheer, emotional conflict |
| **Contrast** | Youth outside vs mother inside | Life vs death, motion vs stillness |

## 🎭 Tone and Mood

- **Tone**: Reflective, melancholic, restrained
- **Mood**: Tender, sorrowful, nostalgic, suppressed grief

---

## 📐 Structure

- **Form**: Single stanza, continuous verse
- **No rhyme/meter**: Free verse makes it conversational and realistic
- **No punctuation**: Creates a breathless, spontaneous flow—mirrors stream of thought

---

## 💡 Interpretation

Kamala Das delicately presents the pain of seeing a parent grow old—a universal experience. The poem is about more than just aging. It captures how people struggle to process love, loss, fear, and separation. The final smile is both an act of love and a mask worn in the face of emotional vulnerability.

---

## 🧠 Critical Insight

Kamala Das doesn’t describe dramatic events. She portrays an *ordinary moment*—a car ride, a goodbye at the airport—but through her eyes, it becomes a deeply emotional experience. Her strength lies in emotional honesty, even if that honesty is masked by a quiet smile.

---

## 📌 Message

- Life is transient.
- Love doesn’t always need words.
- The fear of losing loved ones never truly goes away.
- Sometimes, a smile hides more than it reveals.
