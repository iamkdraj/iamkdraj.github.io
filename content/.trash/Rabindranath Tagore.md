# Rabindranath Tagore

## 1. Biography

*   **Born:** 7 May 1861
*   **Died:** 7 August 1941
*   **Early Life and Education:** Tagore was born in Calcutta, India. He was the youngest of thirteen surviving children. He was largely homeschooled and was trained by his father and older siblings in various subjects. He was sent to England for formal schooling in 1878, but he did not finish his studies there.
*   **Career:** Tagore was a polymath: a poet, writer, playwright, composer, philosopher, social reformer, and painter. He reshaped Bengali literature and music, as well as Indian art with Contextual Modernism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. He became the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913.
*   **Personal Life/Influences:** He was a key figure of the Bengal Renaissance. He was a friend of Mahatma Gandhi and the two had a long and complex relationship.
*   **Mentor/Contemporaries:** He had no formal mentor, but he was influenced by the works of Kalidasa, the Bauls of Bengal, and the Upanishads.

## 3. Detailed Analysis of Major Works

### *Gitanjali*

*   **Plot Summary:** A collection of 103 English prose poems, which are Tagore's own English translations of his Bengali poems.
*   **Themes:** Devotion, love, nature, and the relationship between the human and the divine.
*   **Key Quotes/Lines:** "Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high..."

### *The Home and the World*

*   **Plot Summary:** The novel is set in the early 20th century and explores the conflict between Western culture and Indian nationalism.
*   **Major Characters:**
    *   Nikhil: A wealthy, progressive landlord.
    *   Bimala: Nikhil's wife.
    *   Sandip: A charismatic and radical nationalist leader.
*   **Themes:** Nationalism, tradition vs. modernity, love, and betrayal.

## 4. Literary Style and Themes

*   **Overall Writing Style:** Lyrical, spiritual, and philosophical.
*   **Recurring Themes:** Nationalism, spirituality, humanism, internationalism, and nature.
*   **Contribution to Indian English Literature:** He is considered the father of modern Indian literature.

## 5. Awards and Recognition

*   **Nobel Prize in Literature:** 1913 for *Gitanjali*.
