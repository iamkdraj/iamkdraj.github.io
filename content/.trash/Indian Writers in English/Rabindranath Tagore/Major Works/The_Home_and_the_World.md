# The Home and the World (Ghare Baire)

*   **Plot Summary:** The novel is set in the early 20th century, during the Swadeshi movement. It tells the story of Bimala, a traditional Hindu wife, who is torn between her husband, Nikhil, a wealthy and liberal landlord, and his friend, Sandip, a charismatic and radical nationalist leader.
*   **Major Themes:** The novel explores the conflict between tradition and modernity, the complexities of nationalism, and the nature of love and marriage. It also examines the role of women in a changing society.
*   **Key Characters:**
    *   **Bimala:** The protagonist, who represents the emerging modern Indian woman.
    *   **Nikhil:** Bimala's husband, who represents a more moderate and rational approach to nationalism.
    *   **Sandip:** Nikhil's friend, who represents a more extreme and passionate form of nationalism.
*   **Literary Style:** The novel is written in a realistic and psychological style. Tagore uses the first-person narrative to provide insights into the thoughts and feelings of the three main characters.
