# Rabindranath Tagore

## 1. Biography

*   **Born:** 7 May 1861
*   **Died:** 7 August 1941
*   **Early Life and Education:** Tagore was born in Calcutta, India. He was the youngest of thirteen surviving children. He was educated at home and at the age of seventeen, he was sent to England for formal schooling, but he did not finish his studies there.
*   **Career:** Tagore was a poet, writer, playwright, composer, philosopher, social reformer and painter. He reshaped Bengali literature and music, as well as Indian art with Contextual Modernism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
*   **Personal Life/Influences:** He was a key figure in the Bengal Renaissance. He started composing art at a very young age.
*   **Mentor/Contemporaries:** He had no specific mentor but was influenced by the literary discussions with his siblings and other literary personalities of that time.

## 3. Detailed Analysis of Major Works

### Gitanjali

*   **Plot Summary:** It is a collection of 157 poems. The poems are largely metaphorical and deal with themes of love, spirituality, and nature.
*   **Major Characters:** Not applicable
*   **Themes:** Spirituality, devotion, love, nature.
*   **Key Quotes/Lines:** "Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high..."

## 4. Literary Style and Themes

*   **Overall Writing Style:** Lyrical, spiritual, and philosophical. His writing is known for its simplicity and depth.
*   **Recurring Themes:** Mysticism, humanism, internationalism, and social reform.
*   **Literary Devices:** Metaphor, symbolism, and personification.
*   **Contribution to Indian English Literature:** He was the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature. He is credited with modernizing Bengali literature.

## 5. Awards and Recognition

*   **Nobel Prize in Literature:** 1913 for Gitanjali
