### 13\. Which is not an essential feature of satire?
> [!tip]- (1) It is intended to display tragedy ✅ Correct. This is **not a feature**. Satire is a form of comedy that uses humor, irony, and ridicule to criticize folly and vice. It is fundamentally different from tragedy, which deals with suffering and downfall.

### 127. Change from Direct to Indirect sentence -
He said, “I’ve forgotten the combination of my safe.”

> [!tip]- (1) He told us that he did forgetfully his combination of safe.
> ❌ Incorrect. This is grammatically malformed and changes the verb tense incorrectly.

> [!tip]- (2) Forgot the combination of the safe, ‘he said’!
> ❌ Incorrect. This is not a correctly formed reported sentence.

> [!tip]- (3) The combination of the safe has been forgotten.
> ❌ Incorrect. This changes the voice to passive and loses the original agent ("he").

> [!tip]- (4) He said that he had forgotten the combination of his safe.
> ✅ Correct. This sentence correctly follows the rules of reported speech: the reporting verb `said` is followed by `that`; the tense is backshifted from Present Perfect (`I've forgotten`) to Past Perfect (`he had forgotten`); and the pronoun `my` is changed to `his`.

> To convert a sentence from direct to indirect speech when the reporting verb is in the past:
> 1.  **Tense Backshift:** The Present Perfect (`have/has + past participle`) changes to the Past Perfect (`had + past participle`). So, `have forgotten` becomes `had forgotten`.
> 2.  **Pronoun Change:** Pronouns are changed to reflect the perspective of the reporter. `I` becomes `he`, and `my` becomes `his`.
> Option (4) correctly applies all of these rules.
