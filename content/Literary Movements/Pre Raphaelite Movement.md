# The Pre-Raphaelite Movement

The Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood (PRB) was a revolutionary group of young English painters, poets, and art critics founded in **1848**. The movement began in the visual arts but had a profound impact on Victorian poetry and design. It was a rebellion against the dull, academic art of their time, which they believed was based on a slavish imitation of the High Renaissance master, **Raphael**. They wanted to return to the art of the **early Italian Renaissance (before Raphael)**, which they admired for its vibrant color, detail, and sincere expression.

---

### Core Principles and Characteristics

*   **Truth to Nature**: This was their most important principle. They advocated for meticulous, almost photographic realism, studying nature attentively to represent it with truthfulness.
*   **Vibrant Color and Sensuousness**: In rebellion against the drab colors of conventional Victorian painting, their works are startlingly bright and colorful. Their poetry is highly **sensuous**, appealing to the senses with vivid descriptions.
*   **Symbolism and Detail**: Despite their realism, their works are heavily symbolic. Every detailed object often carries a deeper moral or religious meaning.
*   **Medievalism and Literary Subjects**: They were fascinated by the medieval past, Arthurian legends, and the works of Dante and Keats. Their art often tells a story.
*   **Fusion of Flesh and Spirit**: The Pre-Raphaelites explored the connection between physical beauty and spiritual love, which was often controversial. This led to the hostile term **"The Fleshly School of Poetry,"** used by critic Robert Buchanan to attack their work as overly sensual and immoral.

---

### Key Figures and Writings

The movement centered around a core group of founders and their associates. They also started a short-lived journal called ***The Germ***.

*   **Dante Gabriel Rossetti (D.G. Rossetti)**: A painter and poet, he was the charismatic leader of the Brotherhood. His work is passionate, sensual, and mystical.
    *   *Key Writings:* ***"The Blessed Damozel,"*** *The House of Life* (sonnet sequence).
*   **Christina Rossetti**: Sister of D.G. Rossetti and a major Victorian poet. Her poetry combines Pre-Raphaelite sensuousness with deep Anglican faith.
    *   *Key Writings:* ***Goblin Market and Other Poems***. The title poem is a rich, symbolic allegory about temptation and redemption.
*   **William Morris**: A poet, artist, designer, and socialist thinker. He championed craftsmanship in reaction against shoddy industrial goods.
    *   *Key Writings:* *The Defence of Guenevere*.
*   **John Everett Millais & William Holman Hunt**: The other two principal founders, primarily painters. Their works, like Millais's *Ophelia*, exemplify the PRB style.
*   **John Ruskin**: Not a member, but the most powerful art critic of the era. He became the PRB's most important champion, praising their principle of "truth to nature."

The Pre-Raphaelite Movement's emphasis on detailed symbolism and sensuousness was a major influence on the later **Aesthetic and Symbolist movements**.
